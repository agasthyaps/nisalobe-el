Determine Themes: *Two Roads* Anchor Chart

(Example for Teacher Reference)

A **topic** is a subject or issue that comes up in the text.

The **theme** is the observation or statement the author is making about that topic.

A theme is the message or observation that an author reveals through a significant and repeated idea in a text. The **themes** of a text are observations that an author is making about life or the “real world” through the text. Themes are rooted in the text and evident throughout a text, but relevant to a broader audience.

Topics explored repeatedly in *Two Roads*:

- **American Indian boarding schools**

- **race and identity**

- **Great Depression**

- **friendship**

- **family**

Emerging Themes in *Two Roads*:

- **Following a code can root a person in their values and guide their life.**

- **Respect all you encounter.**

- **Spending time in nature can help us recharge and find peace.**

- **Family members take care of each other, even when it requires self-sacrifice.**

- **United States history can be biased and incomplete. It is important to consider the perspectives of those who have been left out of popular accounts in history.**

- **It is critical to study indigenous histories so their contributions are not forgotten.**

- **Identities are complicated and conflicting, and tensions may exist between our different identities.**

- **Our peers, our school, our families, and our experiences can affirm or threaten our identities.**

- **People have the power to start change in their country by challenging those in positions of power.**

- **Finding one’s identity often involves recognizing one’s values.**
