Entrance Ticket: Unit 2, Lesson 12

L.6.1d

(Example for Teacher Reference)

**Directions:** As you enter class, read the learning targets. Then, revise each sentence to correct a vague or ambiguous pronoun.

Superintendent Morrell wished the escaping students would give it up.

Sample student responses:

Superintendent Morrell wished the students would give up trying to escape.

Superintendent Morrell wished the students who tried to escape would give it up.

When Cal hit his arm on the boxcar door frame, it broke.

Sample student responses:

When Cal hit his arm on the boxcar door frame, his arm broke.

When Cal hit his arm on the boxcar door frame, the frame broke.

The crowds of marchers pushed past the cavalry soldiers; they were angry.

Sample student responses:

The angry crowds of marchers pushed past the cavalry soldiers.

The crowds of marchers pushed past the cavalry soldiers, who were angry.

The crowds of marchers pushed past the cavalry soldiers; both groups were angry.
