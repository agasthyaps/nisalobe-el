(Example for Teacher Reference)

**Directions:** As you enter class, read the learning targets. Next, complete the following:

Read this sentence from chapter 18 of *Two Roads*.

“**A humorless chuckle** follows his words.”

Using your affix list, record the definition and connotation of **humorless**.

Using a dictionary, record the definition and connotation of **chuckle**.

|           | Definition                          | Connotation  |
|-----------|-------------------------------------|--------------|
| humorless | **without humor; in a serious way** | **negative** |
| chuckle   | **laugh**                           | **positive** |

Based on their definitions and connotations, what do you notice about these two words?

Answers will vary, but may include: The words seem to be opposites. Someone chuckles when something is funny, but humorless means something is not funny. They have opposite connotations as well.
