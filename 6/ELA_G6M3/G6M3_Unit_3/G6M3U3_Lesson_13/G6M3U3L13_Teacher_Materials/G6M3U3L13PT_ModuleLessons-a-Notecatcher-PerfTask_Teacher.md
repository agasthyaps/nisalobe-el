Performance Task: Audio Museum Responses  
and Reflections Note-Catcher

RI.6.1, RI.6.6, W.6.10, SL.6.2

(Example for Teacher Reference)

**Directions:** Visit your classmates’ listening stations and listen to their recordings. For the first three stations you visit, complete the questions below (please note that you will be answering different questions for each recording). Then, complete the final reflection question and be prepared to share your ideas during a discussion.

**Listening Station 1**

Title and author of piece:

Answers will vary, but may include: *My People, the Sioux* by Luther Standing Bear

1.  What is the context of this piece?

Answers will vary, but may include: Luther Standing Bear is a son of a chief. He attended Carlisle Indian School. This piece is from his autobiography.

2.  What are some powerful words and phrases that you heard in this piece? How does this language make you feel?

Answers will vary, but may include: “lonesome”; “if I were only back home”; “thinking of the good times I might be having.” This language makes me feel sad. I can feel the pain of Luther Standing Bear.

**Listening Station 2**

Title and author of piece:

Answers will vary, but may include: *Indian School Days* by Basil Johnston

3.  What are the key ideas of this piece?

Answers will vary, but may include: The students were expected to behave as if in prison. They were awoken by loud noises and expected to line up quickly and silently. The students rebelled in the only way they could without getting into very serious trouble - by purposely moving as slowly as they could.

4.  How does this piece compare or contrast to the piece you recorded or to another of our module texts?

Answers will vary, but may include: In the anchor text, *Two Roads*, Cal is also expected to wake up early, wear a uniform, and march in straight, silent lines. Just like Basil Johnston, Cal and his friends also found ways to bypass the rules, like sneaking out at night to do stomp dances in the forest.

**Listening Station 3**

Title and author of piece:

Answers will vary, but may include: “Drums” by Peter LaFarge

5.  Why is this piece meaningful?

Answers will vary, but may include: This piece is meaningful because it is a poem. It uses beautiful language and rhymes to make an impactful point.

6.  What can we learn from this piece?

Answers will vary, but may include: We can learn that American Indian boarding schools cannot take away people’s identities. Identities are too strong and too important.

**Final Reflection Question**

How does the experience of this audio museum relate to the module as a whole?

Answers will vary, but may include: This audio museum shows the power of voices when people speak together. In this module, we read lots of individual stories of students from American Indian boarding schools. We analyzed individual perspectives and points of view. The audio museum presents all of these different perspectives together. It paints one big picture that holds many experiences within it.
