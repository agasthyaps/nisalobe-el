Language Dive: Model Essay Claim  
Note-Catcher

RI.6.1, W.6.1a, W.6.1c, SL.6.1a

(Example for Teacher Reference)

1.  In your groups, discuss ways to rearrange the chunks to make a meaningful sentence.

Pop should not bring Cal to Washington  
with him.

Although Cal feels nervous and sad that  
he cannot go with his father

Take turns paraphrasing the chunks in order.

Discuss, sketch, or act out: What is this sentence about?

2.  Consider the focus structure.

Although Cal feels nervous and sad that  
he cannot go with his father

What do you want to discuss about this chunk? Refer to the Questions We Can Ask during a Language Dive anchor chart and Conversation Cues anchor chart for support.

3.  Complete this sentence frame to describe something you feel nervous about, and what you are doing or feeling in spite of those nerves.

Although I feel nervous that **I have to write an argument essay**, **I know that I will do my best**.

4.  Complete this sentence frame to reflect on the question you will answer in your collaborative literary argument essays: Should Cal leave Challagi Indian Industrial School to find Pop?

Although **Cal is worried about Pop**, Cal should **stay at Challagi and wait for him**.
