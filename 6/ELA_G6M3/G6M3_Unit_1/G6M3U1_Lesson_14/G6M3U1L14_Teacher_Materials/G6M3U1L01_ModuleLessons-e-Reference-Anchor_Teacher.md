<tr class="odd">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 1</strong></p>
<p>“Keeping Up”</p>
<p>Pages 7–15</p></td>
<td><p><em>Note that this chapter is not read in its entirety due to time constraints.</em></p>
<ul>
<li><p>12-year-old Cal and his father, Pop, have been riding the rails as “knights of the road,” or hoboes, since they lost their farm in the Great Depression and Cal’s mother died.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td rowspan="2"><p><strong>Chapter 2</strong></p>
<p>“Not All That Much”</p>
<p>Pages 16–28</p></td>
<td rowspan="2"><p><em>Note that this chapter is not read in class due to time constraints.</em></p>
<ul>
<li><p>Cal and Pop hold themselves to high standards of conduct, or an ethical code. They stop at houses and offer to work in exchange for food and lodging.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 4</strong></p>
<p>“News”</p>
<p>Pages 38–60</p></td>
<td><p><em>Note that this chapter is not read in its entirety due to time constraints.</em></p>
<ul>
<li><p>Cal and Pop sprint to hop on the next train. Onboard, Pop learns that veterans are marching on Washington, DC to demand their bonus check from their service in the military.</p></li>
<li><p>Pop announces that Cal needs to get back to school, except this time he will be attending Indian school.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 5</strong></p>
<p>“Indian School”</p>
<p>Pages 61–67</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Cal learns that his father is Creek Indian. Pop shares stories of his time as a student at Indian boarding school. Cal struggles with his new identity.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 6</strong></p>
<p>“Hopping Off”</p>
<p>Pages 68–78</p></td>
<td><p><em>Note that this chapter is not read in class due to time constraints.</em></p>
<ul>
<li><p>Cal and Pop make their way to a hobo camp in the forest where Pop is reunited with some of his fellow soldiers. Hoboes use hidden symbols on signposts to indicate which towns are friendly towards “knights of the road” and which are dangerous.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 7</strong></p>
<p>“A Fine Mulligan”</p>
<p>Pages 79–95</p></td>
<td><p><em>Note that this chapter is not read in class due to time constraints.</em></p>
<ul>
<li><p>Pop explains that he kept his Indian ancestry hidden from Cal to protect Cal from bigotry. He explains that he intends to march on Washington with other veterans and that Cal will be safer if he enrolls at Challagi boarding school instead.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 8</strong></p>
<p>“Gunshots”</p>
<p>Pages 96–102</p></td>
<td><p><em>Note that this chapter is not read in class due to time constraints.</em></p>
<ul>
<li><p>The sheriff arrives in camp and forces all of the hoboes to leave. He also mentions that he makes money from catching runaways from Challagi and returning them to school.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 9</strong></p>
<p>“Upside Down”</p>
<p>Pages 103–114</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Pop shares some of his memories of Indian boarding school—some good, some bad. He hopes that the school will have improved since the publication of the Meriam Report.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 10</strong></p>
<p>“Horses”</p>
<p>Pages 115–119</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Cal and Pop demonstrate their talent for handling horses. Their help wrangling the horses earns them a meal.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 11</strong></p>
<p>“An Indian Handshake”</p>
<p>Pages 120–127</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Pop teaches Cal about survival at Indian school—march properly, eat quickly, and fight fairly.</p></li>
<li><p>Cal continues to struggle with his half-Indian identity.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 12</strong></p>
<p>“Under the Arch”</p>
<p>Pages 129–145</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Cal and Pop arrive at Challagi and meet Superintendent Morrell who remembers Pop as a student.</p></li>
<li><p>Cal watches the students do marching drills and grows more nervous.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 13</strong></p>
<p>“The Tour”</p>
<p>Pages 146–149</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Cal learns about discipline at Challagi. Students have daily chores; one is locked in a wooden box for punishment.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 14</strong></p>
<p>“Possum”</p>
<p>Pages 150–155</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Cal meets Possum, a fellow student. He gives Cal his own nickname, Jay Bird, to tease him for being so quiet.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 15</strong></p>
<p>“Infirmary”</p>
<p>Pages 156–163</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>The school nurse roughly cuts Cal’s hair, injects him with vaccines, and gives him a physical exam.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 16</strong></p>
<p>“Good Advice”</p>
<p>Pages 164–176</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Cal begins a friendship with Possum. Possum teaches him some Indian words; Cal shares his books.</p></li>
<li><p>Possum shows Cal a safe place to store his valuables to protect them from school staff.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 17</strong></p>
<p>“The Dorm”</p>
<p>Pages 177–183</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Cal is assigned a bed. He returns to Pop to say goodbye.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 18</strong></p>
<p>“New Duds”</p>
<p>Pages 184–201</p></td>
<td><p><em>Note that an excerpt of this chapter is read during the End of Unit 1 Assessment. Students should <u>not</u> read this chapter prior to the assessment.</em></p>
<ul>
<li><p>Cal meets Skinny, who gives him his school uniforms.</p></li>
<li><p>Cal is approached by a gang of boys who pick a fight with him. The fight is broken up but they don’t tattle on each other; Cal is accepted into the group.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 19</strong></p>
<p>“Mess”</p>
<p>Pages 202–206</p></td>
<td><p><em>Note that this chapter is not read in class due to time constraints.</em></p>
<ul>
<li><p>A meal is served; Cal remembers that he needs to grab food quickly and ruthlessly to survive.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 20</strong></p>
<p>“In Step”</p>
<p>Pages 207–216</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Cal is starting to learn the ways of the school—getting up early, cleaning the latrines, and obeying orders.</p></li>
<li><p>Cal is invited to join the track team.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 21</strong></p>
<p>“Expecting Indians”</p>
<p>Pages 217–226</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Cal witnesses students being treated differently depending on how much they identify as Indian or white.</p></li>
<li><p>The quality of education at the school depends on the class; some students attend just for the first-rate farming classes.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 22</strong></p>
<p>“Another Day at Work”</p>
<p>Pages 227–241</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Cal writes a letter to his father but can’t send it because he doesn’t know where his father is.</p></li>
<li><p>He discovers he has money on his account and buys new shoes in town.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 23</strong></p>
<p>“To Help Indians”</p>
<p>Pages 242–249</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Students at Challagi do hours of back-breaking labor every day.</p></li>
<li><p>Cal takes a beating during boxing practice.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 24</strong></p>
<p>“Stomp Dance”</p>
<p>Pages 250–259</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Cal learns about his Creek identity from his friends at school. They teach him stomp dances and songs.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 25</strong></p>
<p>“First Letter”</p>
<p>Pages 260–268</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Cal enjoys working with the horses at school.</p></li>
<li><p>Cal finally hears from his father who, with other veterans, has set up a tent city in Washington, DC.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 26</strong></p>
<p>“A Bad Dream”</p>
<p>Pages 269–276</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Pop asks the school to keep Cal over the summer.</p></li>
<li><p>Cal has a vision that something terrible will happen to his father.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 27</strong></p>
<p>“Time to Go”</p>
<p>Pages 277–285</p></td>
<td><p><em>Note that this chapter is read during the Mid-Unit 2 Assessment. Students should <u>not</u> read this chapter prior to the assessment.</em></p>
<ul>
<li><p>Deacon and the gang build a sweat lodge.</p></li>
<li><p>The boys help Cal steal a horse so he can find and save his father.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 28</strong></p>
<p>“One Hand on the Rail”</p>
<p>Pages 289–291</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Cal escapes the school and hops on a train to Washington, DC.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 29</strong></p>
<p>“Wounds”</p>
<p>Pages 292–299</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Cal travels for two days on the train and learns that his vision is correct, the government troops have attacked the protesting veterans.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 30</strong></p>
<p>“Tanks on Pennsylvania Avenue”</p>
<p>Pages 300–314</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Calvary are attacking and setting fire to the tent city.</p></li>
<li><p>Cal finds Pop and tells him everything.</p></li>
<li><p>Everyone is driven out of DC.</p></li>
<li><p>Cal decides to return to Challagi.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
