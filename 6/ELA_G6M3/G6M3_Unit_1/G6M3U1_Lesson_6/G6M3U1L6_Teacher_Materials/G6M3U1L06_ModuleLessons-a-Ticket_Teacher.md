**Directions:** As you enter class, read the learning targets. Then, reread the following excerpt from *Two Roads*, and answer the questions below. In this excerpt, the author, Joseph Bruchac, is explaining American Indian boarding schools.

“These schools were largely paramilitary institutions in which Native students were **stripped of** their native languages and cultural identities, drilled, dressed in uniforms, and separated from their families for years at a time” (317).

What is the connotation of **stripped of** as it is used in this sentence (negative, neutral, positive)?

negative

How does the connotation of **stripped of** help to convey Bruchac’s point of view toward American Indian boarding schools? Explain your answer.

Sample student response: The negative connotation of “stripped of” implies that Native students were treated forcefully. Bruchac’s point of view toward the schools is that they operated like the military and treated students harshly.
