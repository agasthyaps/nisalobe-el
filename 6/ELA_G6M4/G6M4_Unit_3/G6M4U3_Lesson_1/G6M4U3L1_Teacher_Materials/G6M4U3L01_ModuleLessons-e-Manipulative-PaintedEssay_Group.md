| When recalling America’s race to put an astronaut on the moon, history often overlooks Dorothy Vaughan. She, along with the other women of the segregated West Area Computing Unit, worked on projects that made airplanes faster and more stable, which helped the United States during World War II. Then she was promoted to supervisor of the West Computers. She led the women whose work would make space travel possible. |
