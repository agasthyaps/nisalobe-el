Synopsis: *Hidden Figures*, Chapter 8

**Name:** **Date:**

- Dorothy was only able to travel back to Farmville, Virginia, during the holidays to see her children.

- Despite the love for her job, Dorothy truly missed her children, so she made arrangements for her kids to join her in Newport News.

- Newsome Park was a community with many family-friendly amenities.

- A year after Dorothy left her children in Farmville to join the work at Langley, she moved her children into an apartment in Newsome Park.

- People began to question what would happen to a successful war town when there was no war.

- August 14, 1945, marked the end of World War II, and the citizens of Newport News celebrated.

- Businesses began to decrease their workforce in order to make room for the servicemen who were headed back home from the war and would need jobs.

- Some businesses reverted back to their discriminatory ways of not hiring African Americans.

- Dorothy continued with her daily routine even though she was not sure how long her job would last.

- The local and federal governments wanted to get rid of Newsome Park, the 2,000 homes built for African American families.

- Newsome Park residents battled back and forth with the government officials about the community they had grown to love.
