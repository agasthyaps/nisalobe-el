<tr class="odd">
<td><p>What are her remarkable accomplishments?</p>
<ul>
<li><p><strong>One of the first African American women to be hired by NACA as a computer.</strong></p></li>
<li><p><strong>Work at Langley was extremely demanding on her and her family.</strong></p></li>
<li><p><strong>First black woman to be promoted to a management position at NACA.</strong></p></li>
</ul></td>
<td><p>Why are these accomplishments remarkable?</p>
<ul>
<li><p><strong>Racial discrimination was common, limiting, and oppressive; she persevered.</strong></p></li>
<li><p><strong>Few role models available because she was one of the first to be hired.</strong></p></li>
<li><p><strong>Could easily have given up from exhaustion and stress.</strong></p></li>
<li><p><strong>Gender discrimination was common, limiting, and oppressive.</strong></p></li>
<li><p><strong>Took the initiative to continue her education and make herself the best candidate for the job.</strong></p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>

<th colspan="2">Mary Jackson</th>
</tr>
</thead>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td><p>What are her remarkable accomplishments?</p>
<ul>
<li><p><strong>Worked with the Supersonic Pressure Tunnel to test airplanes built to fly faster than sound.</strong></p></li>
<li><p><strong>Co-authored a report in 1958 entitled “Effects on Nose Angle and Mach Number on Transition on Cones at Supersonic Speeds” (116).</strong></p></li>
<li><p><strong>Organized a joint lecture with a white colleague about “The Aspects of Engineering for Women.”</strong></p></li>
</ul></td>
<td><p>Why are these accomplishments remarkable?</p>
<ul>
<li><p><strong>America was in a race against Russia to be the first to build an airplane that could fly faster than sound; her work was assisting in a global political rivalry.</strong></p></li>
<li><p><strong>Her research was used to design missiles.</strong></p></li>
<li><p><strong>Lecture took place in Virginia, considered the worst state for segregation.</strong></p></li>
<li><p><strong>Lecture invited girls into the engineering field where few opportunities for women were available.</strong></p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>

<th colspan="2">Katherine Johnson</th>
</tr>
</thead>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td><p>What are her remarkable accomplishments?</p>
<ul>
<li><p><strong>One of the first students to desegregate a graduate school in West Virginia.</strong></p></li>
<li><p><strong>Research led to changes in air traffic regulations that made air travel safer.</strong></p></li>
<li><p><strong>Finally allowed to attend the meetings and lectures of the Aerospace Mechanics Division of NASA.</strong></p></li>
</ul></td>
<td><p>Why are these accomplishments remarkable?</p>
<ul>
<li><p><strong>No graduate programs for black students available at the time; bravely pioneered this path for other black students.</strong></p></li>
<li><p><strong>Air traffic regulations were brand new; airplane passengers are much safer now as a result.</strong></p></li>
<li><p><strong>Women were not allowed in these meetings; meetings gave her access to the latest information about aeronautics.</strong></p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
