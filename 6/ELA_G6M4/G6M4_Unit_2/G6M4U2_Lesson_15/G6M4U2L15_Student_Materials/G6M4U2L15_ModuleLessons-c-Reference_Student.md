Synopsis: *Hidden Figures*, Chapters 22–23

**Name:** **Date:**

**Chapter 22**

- In 1963, A. Philip Randolph led the March on Washington for Jobs and Freedom where Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. gave his “I Have a Dream” speech.

- Dorothy Vaughan received recognition for her twenty years of service to the federal government.

- Despite some progress being made for civil rights, there were still some setbacks. The director at Langley wrote to NASA headquarters lamenting the low number of African American applicants. The director attributed this fact to the continued social segregation in the Hampton area.

- Katherine Johnson approached Christine Mann Darden at church and introduced herself. Christine knew who Katherine was because Christine had attended school with Katherine’s daughters.

- After graduating from the Hampton Institute, Christine married and had children. Christine had been working as a math teacher.

- Christine attended Virginia State University for her graduate degree, and toward the end of her studies, she filled out an application for federal employment.

- NASA hired Christine as a data analyst, which led her to Hampton Roads.

- Katherine and Christine never worked together, but they knew of each other.

- Katherine continued to work on the possibilities of space flight. The tragedy of Apollo I deeply impacted NASA and its employees.

- The Apollo program continued to strive toward placing a man on the moon.

**Chapter 23**

- Prior to the Apollo 11 launch, two hundred protesters marched outside Cape Canaveral. The Poor People’s Campaign was a movement to bring attention to the needs of poor people who needed financial support from the government.

- Many people could not understand how so much money could be spent trying to put someone on the moon when American citizens were struggling to survive.

- On July 20, 1969, six hundred million people around the world watched Neil Armstrong climb down the ladder of the Apollo command module and step onto the moon.

- The astronauts returned safely to Earth, and Langley minds began to wonder about the next challenge in space.
