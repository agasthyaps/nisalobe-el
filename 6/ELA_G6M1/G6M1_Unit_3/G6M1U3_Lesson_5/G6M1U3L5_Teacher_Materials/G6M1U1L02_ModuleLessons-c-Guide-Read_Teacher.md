Text Guide: *The Lightning Thief*

(For Teacher Reference)

The purpose of this text guide is to support students as they read excerpts from *The Lightning Thief* in class. Each section of the vocabulary guide (1) calls out vocabulary words that may pose a particular challenge to students, and (2) features two to three general comprehension questions that can be asked to gauge student understanding of the text.

Unit 1

**Lesson 2, Chapter 1, Pages 8–15**

Read from page 8 (“The class gathered on the front steps . . .”) to the end of the chapter on page 15, pausing to define the following vocabulary and ask the following questions as needed.

**Vocabulary**

- pulverize (11): destroy completely

- vaporized (13): changed from something physical to a gas

**Questions**

- page 11, “I wasn’t so sure”

**How does Nancy Bobofit end up in the fountain? Why is Percy confused about what happened? Nancy thinks Percy pushed her, but it seems like something might have grabbed her from the water. Percy is confused because he has memory lapses sometimes, when his “brain falls asleep.”**

- page 12, “Then things got even stranger”

**What do we know about Mrs. Dodds? What do we know about Mrs. Dodd’s relationship to Percy? Mrs. Dodds is not human. She thinks Percy is hiding a secret and wants him to confess.**

- page 15, end of chapter

**What do we know about Percy so far? Percy is confused. He sees things that aren’t really happening. He might be unknowingly involved in something dangerous.**

**Vocabulary**

- flanked (122): with others on the left and right side

**Questions**

- page 108, “Why couldn’t my dad, whoever he was, make a phone appear?”

**What is Percy waiting for? What is he doing in the meantime? Percy is waiting for his father, who is a god, to signal that Percy is his son. In the meantime, he is getting used to camp activities. He is especially talented at canoeing.**

- page 126, end of chapter

**Who is Percy’s father? How did Percy learn this? Percy’s father is Poseidon, the Sea God. He learned this because his wounds healed when he was standing in a creek. Soon after, a trident symbolizing Poseidon appeared over his head.**

**Lesson 1, Chapter 13, Pages 203–211**

**Vocabulary**

- gnashing (208): striking or grinding teeth together, as in anger

- seared (208): burned or scorched the outside of

- glittered (210): shined brightly, sparkled

- plummeted (211): fell sharply down, often at high speed

**Questions**

- page 206, “I was ready to go pretty quick”

**Why is Percy feeling anxious? It is dark and he is worried that Hades is nearby. He stands in an elevator and feels claustrophobic. Then, he is high up on an observation deck and he is afraid of heights.**

- page 209, “Great, I thought. We just blowtorched a national monument”

**What has happened to Percy since Annabeth and Grover left on the elevator? He realized that the strange woman on the observation deck is Echidna, the Mother of Monsters, and her dog is really a dangerous Chimera. The Chimera tries to blow fire at Percy, but it hits the Arch instead.**

- page 211, end of chapter

**How does the chapter end? The Chimera is ready to kill Percy. Echidna dares him to jump into the water to prove his bloodline: if Poseidon is really Percy’s father, Poseidon should be able to protect him in the water. Percy is scared, but he jumps.**

**Vocabulary**

- ultimatum (313): a final statement of demands often delivered with a threat of action

**Questions**

- page 314, “‘But I didn’t!’”

**What does Percy accuse Hades of? Stealing Zeus’s master bolt as a way to start a war among the gods.**

**What does Hades accuse Percy of? Following Poseidon’s orders and stealing Zeus’s master bolt and Hades’ helm of darkness.**

- page 319, end of chapter

**What was in Percy’s backpack, and how did it get there? Zeus’s master bolt was in Percy’s backpack. They don’t know how it got there, but Percy has an idea.**

**What does Percy plan to do next? Get Zeus’s master bolt back to Olympus and talk to the god who had “tricked” him by putting the bolt in his backpack. The chapter doesn’t mention him by name, but Ares was the god who gave Percy the backpack.**

**Questions**
