(For Teacher Reference)

**Directions:** As you enter class, read the learning targets. Next, read the excerpt from *The Lightning Thief*, and answer the following questions.

“Chiron smiled at me sympathetically, the way he used to in Latin class, as if to let me know that no matter what my average was, I was his star student. He expected me to have the right answer.” (66)

What does Percy think of Mr. Brunner in this excerpt from chapter 5? How has the author developed Percy’s point of view toward Mr. Brunner from the previous chapter? QuickWrite your response.

Some words that the author uses in this chapter to show Percy’s point of view toward Mr. Brunner are . . .

In the previous chapter, Percy’s point of view toward Mr. Brunner was . . .

Now, Percy’s point of view toward Mr. Brunner is . . .

I know this because . . .

Answers will vary. Sample student response: As the story goes on and readers learn more about Mr. Brunner, Percy’s point of view of Mr. Brunner changes. At the beginning of the story, Percy thought Mr. Brunner was a cool, interesting teacher. Then he was disappointed with Mr. Brunner because of the way he interpreted Mr. Brunner’s words. Now he knows Mr. Brunner was actually trying to help him and keep him safe at the school, so he has a positive opinion of him again.
