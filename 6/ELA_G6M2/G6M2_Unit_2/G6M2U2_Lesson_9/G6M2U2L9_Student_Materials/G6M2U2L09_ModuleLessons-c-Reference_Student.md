Synopsis: *The Boy Who Harnessed the Wind*, Chapter 14

**Name:** **Date:**

- A teacher at Wimbe Primary asks William to start a science club for the students.

- William creates a small windmill for the school.

- The windmill allows students to listen to the radio as well as charge their parents’ mobile phones.

- William explains the steps he uses to build as he demonstrates for the students.

- Officials from the Malawi Teacher Training Activity visit Wimbe Primary, and the officials notice William’s windmill.

- One of the officials calls Dr. Hartford Mchazime to hear William’s story of the windmill.

- Dr. Mchazime is impressed by William and brings Everson Maseya, a journalist from Radio One, to the Kamkwamba home the next week.

- Dr. Mchazime also brings reporters from *The Daily Times*, *The Nation*, *Malawi News*, and Mudziwithu and Zodiak Radio channels.

- The Radio One interviews aired and *The Daily Times* article is published. Eventually the program director of the TED Global Conference learns of William and asks him to apply to be a fellow.

- Dr. Mchazime returns to the Kamkwamba home and helps William to fill out the TED paperwork.

- While waiting on the TED event date to arrive, William, with the help of Dr. Mchazime, enrolls at a public boarding school, Madisi Secondary.

- The TED event date draws near and, on his flight, William sits next to Soyapi Mumba, the software engineer who was initially responsible for sharing William’s story with Mike McKay. Mike McKay is the man who wrote about William on *Hacktivate*, the blog which led to TED finding William.
