**Directions:** Analyze specific passages from this text excerpt to determine its central idea and how that central idea is conveyed through particular key details. Then, answer the questions during the close read.

1.  Look again at the sentence, “I didn’t have any rope, so I had to borrow my mother’s wire clothesline instead.”

Why do you think this sentence is included?

2.  Reread pages 196–197 aloud starting at “‘Easy now!’” to “little by little it made its way up the tower.”

What is actually happening in this section of text?

3.  Reread the rest of page 197.

What are the last two sentences on this page telling us? Why are they important?

4.  Reread page 198 starting at “While Geoffrey made . . .” and ending at “‘just stand back and watch.’”

What do we learn from the conversation with Kalino?

5.  Reread pages 198–199 starting at “I climbed down” and ending at “I’d prepared for this moment for months.”

6.  Reread pages 199–201 starting at “‘Let’s see” and ending at “. . . burn my hand.”

How has the response of the crowd changed here? How do you know? Why is this important to the story?

7.  What does “my heart nearly exploded” show us about what William is feeling at this point?
