1.  Write an effective summary of chapter 6 of *The Boy Who Harnessed the Wind*. (RI.6.2)

Chapter 6 of *The Boy Who Harnessed the Wind* follows William through his first experience at Kachokolo Secondary School as well as his dropping out from the school.  
A central idea of this chapter is that William takes pride in being a student and regards his education as a necessity. William scrubs his white shirt so his uniform is clean and presentable for school. He competes with other students to provide the correct answer. William even lies to the principal about having proper footwear so that he can continue to attend classes. The Kamkwamba family’s lack of money results in William dropping out of Kachokolo.

The chapter’s structure is cause and effect, and this sentence is the turning point, the moment when William begins to understand the effect of Khamba in his life. The cause of Khamba’s arrival was Uncle Socrates’ difficulties, losing his job and moving back to Wimbe with his daughters and Khamba.
