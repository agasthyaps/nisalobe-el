Synopsis: *The Boy Who Harnessed the Wind*, Chapter 8

(For Teacher Reference)

**Note: This synopsis should not be shared with students since they are reading chapter 8 for the assessment.**

- People contract cholera, and the disease spreads quickly.

- William can see the effects of starvation on his family members.

- The maize crop is growing. William predicts that the crop will be ready to eat in twenty days.

- One day, Geoffrey and William squeeze a few ears of maize and discover the maize is ready to be eaten.

- William cooks the maize directly on the coals and the family enjoys a meal from which they get full stomachs.
