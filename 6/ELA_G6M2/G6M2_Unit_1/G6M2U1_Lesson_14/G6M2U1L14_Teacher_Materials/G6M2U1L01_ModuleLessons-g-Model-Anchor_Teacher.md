<th>Sample Gist Statement</th>

<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td><p><strong>Prologue</strong></p>
<p>Pages 1–3</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>The prologue highlights a key moment when William premieres his invention in front of a doubting crowd and discovers that it works!</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 1</strong></p>
<p>“When Magic Ruled the World”</p>
<p>Pages 4–35</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>William describes the small rural town in Malawi where his family has a farm. He explains the role magic plays in his culture and why he is skeptical of magic.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 2</strong></p>
<p>“Khamba”</p>
<p>Pages 36–44</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>William’s uncle John dies, leaving Geoffrey without a father but with a farm to maintain. William’s uncle Socrates gives William a dog, Khamba, and they become close friends.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 3</strong></p>
<p>“Discovering a Thing Called Science”</p>
<p>Pages 45–61</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>William becomes curious about how things work and decides that he wants to be a scientist. He and Geoffrey spend a lot of time taking things apart and putting them back together, like radios.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 4</strong></p>
<p>“The Uncertain Life of an African Farmer”</p>
<p>Pages 62–68</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>A drought and a lack of fertilizer creates a very small harvest. William worries that they will not have enough food to last the year.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 5</strong></p>
<p>“Malawi Begins to Starve”</p>
<p>Pages 69–98</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Famine creates starvation and desperation in Malawi. William’s family attempts to survive off one tiny meal per day.</p></li>
<li><p>William fights desperate crowds to get a small bag of maize from the government. He resorts to eating boiled goat skin for his Christmas dinner.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 6</strong></p>
<p>“My School Assignment”</p>
<p>Pages 99–113</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>William is excited to start school but is assigned to the worst school in the area. It doesn’t matter though because he can’t afford the school fees and has to drop out.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 7</strong></p>
<p>“A Time of Dying”</p>
<p>Pages 114–127</p></td>
<td><p><em>Note that this chapter is not read in class or for homework due to time constraints and the sensitive nature of its content.</em></p>
<ul>
<li><p>Many people are dying from starvation. To end his dog Khamba’s suffering, William ties him to a tree and lets him die.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 8</strong></p>
<p>“Twenty Days”</p>
<p>Pages 128–136</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Cholera and starvation continue to cause widespread death. As soon as the first corn ripens, William’s family is finally able to eat again. They have survived the worst.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 9</strong></p>
<p>“The Library”</p>
<p>Pages 137–162</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>William discovers textbooks on energy at the small local library. He decides to build a windmill to generate electricity for his family.</p></li>
<li><p>William, Gilbert, and Geoffrey use their research and salvaged items to start building a prototype of a windmill.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 10</strong></p>
<p>“Harvest Time”</p>
<p>Pages 163–186</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Malawi is finally recovering from the famine with a good harvest. William still does not have enough money to pay the school fees. He spends his time tinkering in the scrapyard instead, working on his windmill.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 11</strong></p>
<p>“The Windmill Comes to Life”</p>
<p>Pages 187–207</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>With the help of Geoffrey and Gilbert, William is finally able to complete the construction of his windmill. It works, and William is able to light his bedroom!</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 12</strong></p>
<p>“Bigger and Brighter”</p>
<p>Pages 208–225</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>William uses information from the textbooks to improve on his original windmill design. He figures out how to use step up transformers and pulley systems to make his machines safer.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 13</strong></p>
<p>“The Restless Inventor”</p>
<p>Pages 226–241</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>William challenges himself to try building new machines. He has several failures. Famine returns to Malawi and his community blames the windmill.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 14</strong></p>
<p>“The World Discovers Wimbe”</p>
<p>Pages 242–259</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>William’s windmill gets international attention from journalists. William is rewarded with enrollment at a special school and an invitation to speak at a TED conference in Tanzania.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><p><strong>Chapter 15</strong></p>
<p>“Meeting Ted and Tim”</p>
<p>Pages 260–278</p></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Donations from TED allow William to attend a better school, bring electricity to his entire village, and improve his family’s living conditions.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><p><strong>Epilogue</strong></p>
<p>Pages 279–290</p></td>
<td><p><em>Note that students are encouraged to read the epilogue for homework, but it is not discussed in class.</em></p>
<ul>
<li><p>William earns scholarships to a prestigious high school in Africa and to Dartmouth College in New Hampshire. After graduation he starts the Moving Windmills project to use his science and engineering skills to help other villages in Africa.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
