<td><strong>What?</strong></td>
<td>The witch doctor is called <em>sing’anga</em>—uses bones, spices, and powders as cures for ailments.</td>

<tr class="even">
<td><p>William believes Mister<br />
Phiri gets his strength from <em>mangolomera</em>. When William tries it himself to improve his soccer skills, he realizes he’s been cheated.</p>
<p>William equates science<br />
with “fact and reason”; he equates traditional magic with “mystery and hocus-pocus” (35).</p></td>
<td><p><strong>So What?</strong></p>
<p>William is skeptical of magic and the way it shapes the thinking of his friends and family.</p></td>
<td><p>Believing in magic caused problems “especially if a person was truly sick” (7).</p>
<p>Villagers died from treatable illnesses, like diarrhea, from trusting too much in magic cures.</p></td>
</tr>
