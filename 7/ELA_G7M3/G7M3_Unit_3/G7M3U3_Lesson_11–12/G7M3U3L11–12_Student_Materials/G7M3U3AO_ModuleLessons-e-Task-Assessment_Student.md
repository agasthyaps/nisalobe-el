**Name:** **Date:**

**Directions:** In this assessment, you will present your curator’s statement, one piece from the exhibit of works from the Harlem Renaissance, a visual of that piece, and the associated label for that one piece. You will also include a visual or multimedia display in the form of an artwork, audio, or video from the Harlem Renaissance. You will be assessed on your presentation skills (SL.7.4, SL.7.5, SL.7.6), as well as your capacity to draw connections between the several texts and art forms in your exhibit. Your presentation will be about 2–4 minutes in length.

An effective museum exhibit presentation will

- include a curator’s statement,

- include a piece from the Harlem Renaissance and its associated label,

- include a visual display,

- use formal English and standard English conventions to help others understand the content, and

- include academic and domain-specific vocabulary when appropriate.

An effective presenter will

- present your curator’s statement and label, explaining how the piece you have chosen connects to the theme in a focused, coherent manner using pertinent descriptions, facts, details, and examples;

- speak in a formal, clear manner, emphasizing important points;

- speak with adequate volume;

- speak with clear pronunciation;

- use appropriate eye contact while speaking;

- speak at a pace that is easy to follow; and

- directly refer to the visual display to emphasize and clarify points.
