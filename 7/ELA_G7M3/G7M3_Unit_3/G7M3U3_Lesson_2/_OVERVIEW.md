# Lesson 2: Analyze Structure, Language, and Theme: “On Bully Patrol,” Part I
**Grade 7: Module 3: Unit 3: Lesson 2**

**Summary:** Students read Nikki Grimes’ poem “On Bully Patrol” and the Harlem Renaissance poem from which it borrows—“Hope” by Georgia Douglas Johnson. Students analyze the first half of “On Bully Patrol” for its Golden Shovel structure, figurative language, and theme. Students further explore the Golden Shovel

**Focus Standards:** RL.7.4, RL.7.5

**Learning Targets:**
- I can analyze how the structure of a poem contributes to its meaning. (RL.7.4, RL.7.5)
- I can determine the meaning of figurative language in “On Bully Patrol.” (RL.7.4)

**Agenda:** Opening → Engage the Learner – RL.7.4 (5 minutes) → Work Time → Read and Analyze Structure: “On Bully Patrol” – RL.7.5 (20 minutes) → Analyze Figurative Language: “On Bully Patrol” – RL.7.4 (10 minutes) → Closing and Assessment
