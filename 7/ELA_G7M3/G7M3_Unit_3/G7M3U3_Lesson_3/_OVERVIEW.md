# Lesson 3: Analyze Structure, Language, and Theme: “On Bully Patrol,” Part II
**Grade 7: Module 3: Unit 3: Lesson 3**

**Summary:** Students continue their comparative analysis of Nikki Grimes’ poem “On Bully Patrol” and the Harlem Renaissance poem from which it borrows—“Hope” by Georgia Douglas Johnson. Students analyze the second half of “On Bully Patrol” for its Golden Shovel structure, figurative language, and theme.

**Focus Standards:** RL.7.2, RL.7.4, RL.7.5

**Learning Targets:**
- I can analyze how the structure of “On Bully Patrol” contributes to its meaning. (RL.7.4, RL.7.5)
- I can determine the meaning of figurative language in “On Bully Patrol.” (RL.7.4)
- I can determine a theme and trace its development over the course of “On Bully Patrol.” (RL.7.2)

**Agenda:** Opening → Engage the Learner – W.7.5 (5 minutes) → Work Time → Read and Analyze Structure: “On Bully Patrol” – RL.7.5 (20 minutes) → Analyze Figurative Language: “On Bully Patrol” – RL.7.4 (10 minutes) → Closing and Assessment
