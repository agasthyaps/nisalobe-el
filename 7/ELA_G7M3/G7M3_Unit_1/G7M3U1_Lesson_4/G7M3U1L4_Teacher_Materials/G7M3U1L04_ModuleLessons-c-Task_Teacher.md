Compare Text and Music:  
“I’m Just Wild about Harry”

RL.7.4, RL.7.7, L.7.5

(Answers for Teacher Reference)

**Text**

1.  Read the following excerpt:

“He’s sweet just like choc’late candy / and just like honey from the bee” How does this figurative language convey Jessie’s opinion of Harry? (RL.7.4, L.7.5)

2.  <u>He is wonderful.</u>

3.  He gives nice gifts.

4.  He looks nice but acts mean.

5.  He is irresistible but bad for her.

6.  This question has two parts. First, answer Part A. Then answer Part B.

**Part A**

Read the following excerpt and underline the phrase that best helps you understand the meaning of the word **ecstasy**. (RL.7.4)

The heav’nly blisses of his kisses / Fill me with ecstasy.

The **<u>heav’nly blisses</u>** of his kisses / Fill me with ecstasy.

**Part B**

What does **ecstasy** mean in this context? (RL.7.4)

a feeling of great pleasure or joy

**Music**

7.  What is the mood or overall feeling of the song? What techniques does the singer use to create this mood? (RL.7.7)

It is fast-paced, has an upbeat mood. The singers alter their voices to show happiness and excitement.

8.  How does the song version differ from the text? (RL.7.7)

There are two singers instead of one. Also, the singer sings “ecstasy” and “bee” with high notes, but the text doesn’t indicate this change.

9.  How does having two people sing the song affect the meaning of the words? (RL.7.7)

10. <u>It shows their mutual love for each other.</u>

11. It shows their different opinions of each other.

12. It shows why Jessie must keep a secret from Harry.

13. It allows them to tell the story of how Harry and Jessie met.

14. How does the way Jessie sings the last part of the song a second time affect the meaning? (RL.7.7)

15. <u>It makes the words even stronger by adding enthusiasm.</u>

16. It brings the theme into question by singing more quietly.

17. It helps explain how Harry has reacted to Jessie in the past.

18. It allows her the chance to express a different view of Harry.
