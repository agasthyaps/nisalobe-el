# Lesson 5: Read and Analyze “The Boy and the Bayonet,” Part II
**Grade 7: Module 3: Unit 2: Lesson 5**

**Summary:** Students read the second part of “The Boy and the Bayonet,” independently analyzing how story elements interact and how the author develops and contrasts points of view. Next, students analyze the themes and their development as well as questions they have about the story.

**Focus Standards:** RL.7.3, RL.7.6

**Learning Targets:**
- I can explain how the plot shapes the characters in “The Boy and the Bayonet.” (RL.7.3)
- I can identify the points of view of Bud, Hannah, and “little sister” in “The Boy and the Bayonet.” (RL.7.6)
- I can explain how an author develops and contrasts the points of view of Bud, Hannah, and “little sister” in “The Boy and the Bayonet.” (RL.7.6)

**Agenda:** Opening → Engage the Learner – L.7.4 (5 minutes) → Work Time → Read “The Boy and the Bayonet” – RL.7.3 (20 minutes) → Analyze “The Boy and the Bayonet” – RL.7.6 (15 minutes) → Closing and Assessment
