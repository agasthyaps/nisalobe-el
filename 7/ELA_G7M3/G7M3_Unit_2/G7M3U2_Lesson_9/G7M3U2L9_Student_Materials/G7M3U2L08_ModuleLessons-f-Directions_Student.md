Write a Pair Argument Essay

Throughout this unit, you will work with a partner to plan and write an essay to address this prompt: **As a curator, you have decided to create a Harlem Renaissance exhibit focusing on the theme that collaboration and community can bring out the best in people. Choose three works for the collection that most powerfully illustrate this theme, and write an argument essay defending your claim about why these works are such a powerful illustration of this theme.**

REMEMBER: A well-written, argument essay

- clearly states a main claim and stays focused throughout the essay;

- acknowledges a counterclaim;

- uses accurate and relevant examples, details, and quotations to support your points;

- explains the evidence and reasoning;

- groups information logically, in a way that makes your thinking clear to the reader;

- follows rules of writing (spelling, punctuation, and grammar); and

- uses a formal style.

Use the following documents to assist you in writing your essay:

- Texts and Artwork from Module 3, Units 1 and 2: *Shuffle Along*, “Lift Every Voice and Sing,” “Calling Dreams,” “Hope,” “I Shall Return,” *Ethiopia Awakening*, *African Phantasy: Awakening*, “The Negro Speaks of Rivers,” “His Motto,” and “The Boy and the Bayonet”

- Argument Writing Plan graphic organizer

- Vocabulary log (from Module 1, Unit 1, Lesson 2, Work Time A)

- Online or print dictionaries (including ELL and home language dictionaries)
