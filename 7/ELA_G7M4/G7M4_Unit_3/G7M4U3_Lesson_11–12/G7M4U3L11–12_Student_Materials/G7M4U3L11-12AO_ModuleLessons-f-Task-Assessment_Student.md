The purpose of the pitch is to explain your project and generate excitement in the audience of your peers. Your presentation must include multimedia and/or visuals in the form of your storyboard.

An effective documentary pitch will include

- an overview of the clip and an explanation of why you are making it,

- a description of some of the key scenes using the storyboard as reference, and

- a closing statement reiterating the importance of making the documentary clip.

An effective documentary pitch presentation will

- present claims and findings and emphasize points in a coherent way,

- use descriptions, facts, details, and examples to explain and support claims and findings;

- use formal English and standard English conventions to help others understand the content, and

- include academic and domain-specific vocabulary when appropriate.

An effective presenter will

- speak in a formal, clear manner, emphasizing important points;

- speak with adequate volume,

- speak with clear pronunciation,

- use appropriate eye contact while speaking,

- speak at a pace that is easy to follow, and

- directly refer to the storyboard to emphasize and clarify points.
