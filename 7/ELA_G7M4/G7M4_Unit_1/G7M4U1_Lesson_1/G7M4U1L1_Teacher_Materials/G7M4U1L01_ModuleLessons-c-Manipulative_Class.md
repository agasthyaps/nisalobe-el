Scavenger Hunt Resources: Classroom Version

RI.7.1

**Note to Teacher:** Cut apart the Task and Information cards. Post the cards around the room. Write the text in large letters on chart paper to make it easy to read.

<tr class="odd">
<td><p>Task 1</p>
<p>Study the garbage can and the recycling bin (if your classroom has one). Has anything been put in the garbage that belongs in the recycling? Draw any items on your note-catcher.</p></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><p>Task 2</p>
<p>Look around the classroom. Count the number of single-use plastic water bottles you find. (Ask permission before looking in someone’s personal items.) Write that number on your note-catcher, and take a photograph of one single-use water bottle that you find.</p></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><p>Task 3</p>
<p>Look around the classroom. Count the number of single-use plastic containers (of any kind) you find. (Ask permission before looking in someone’s personal items.) Write that number on your note-catcher, and take a photograph of one single-use plastic container that you find.</p></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><p>Task 4</p>
<p>Study the furniture, storage, and items in your classroom. Create a note-catcher and document the five most common materials that items in your classroom are made from. Study the note-catcher, and write your notices and wonders.</p></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><p>Task 5</p>
<p>Look at the tags on your clothing. If you need help, ask a friend. Be respectful of others’ personal space, and ask for consent to check a friend’s tag. Are any of your clothing items made from plastic? (Hint: the following clothing materials are plastic: polyester, nylon, acrylic, and polyamide.) Take a video documenting the results of your analysis.</p></td>
</tr>
