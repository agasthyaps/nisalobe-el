<td><p><strong>Guiding Questions</strong></p>
<p>(Note: These will be recorded at the beginning of the module and revisited throughout the module as students learn new answers to them.)</p>
<ul>
<li><p>Where and how does plastic pollute?</p></li>
<li><p>What can be done about plastic pollution? What is being done? What can I do?</p></li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Big Ideas</strong></p>
<p>(Note: These will be recorded throughout the module as students discover them.)</p>
<ul>
<li><p>Plastic pollutes our world (especially our oceans) when people dispose of it incorrectly.</p></li>
<li><p>Plastic pollution travels from land to the ocean and is taken by the currents in a big vortex called the North Pacific Gyre.</p></li>
<li><p>Animals and people are being harmed by plastic pollution through the consumption of plastics.</p></li>
<li><p>Plastic entered our world in the 1950s, and, through marketing and lifestyle, has become ubiquitous.</p></li>
<li><p>There are three main points in the life cycle of plastic: the beginning (making plastic), the middle (buying and using plastic), and the end (disposing of plastic). People try to combat plastic pollution with different solutions at these three points in the plastic life cycle.</p></li>
</ul></td>
