**Directions:** In the Model Podcast Script, reread the third section, titled “Tools, Mindset, and Character.” Answer the following questions to practice analyzing the interactions of individuals, events, and ideas.

1.  Who were some the individuals involved in the 2002 plague?

Answers will vary, but may include: general public, people infected with the plague and others they came in contact with, government officials, scientists, and healthcare workers.

2.  How did the individuals react to the 2002 plague?

Answers will vary, but may include: they raced to find a solution; had a plan; stopped the plague in its tracks; sent scientists to the source; used the shoe-leather method to gather information and identify patient zero; gave antibiotics; fumigated villages to kill lice; conducted a public campaign about how the plague spread and can be treated and prevented.

3.  Which medical ideas about the plague were most likely influencing the actions of these individuals? Underline all that apply.

4.  The plague was extremely deadly.

5.  The plague was extremely contagious.

6.  People had to leave the country to prevent the plague.

7.  People had to leave the country to find cures for the plague.

8.  <u>The plague could be cured with medicines such as antibiotics.</u>

9.  <u>The plague could be controlled by understanding information.</u>

10. The plague could be prevented by avoiding contact with others.

11. Which social ideas about the plague were most likely influencing the actions of these individuals? Underline all that apply.

12. Rich people could not contract the plague.

13. Poor people were the original source of the plague.

14. <u>Rats and fleas were the original source of the plague.</u>

15. Poor people should be responsible for caring for the rich during an outbreak.

16. Rich people should be responsible for caring for the poor during an outbreak.

17. People should only take care of themselves and their families during an outbreak.

18. <u>People should work together to take care of all sick and dying during an outbreak.</u>
