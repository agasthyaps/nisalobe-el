(Answers for Teacher Reference)

**Directions:** Reread your responses to the homework in Lessons 2 and 3 on Analyze Interactions: Model Podcast Script, Parts I and II. Answer the following questions to practice analyzing the interactions of individuals, events, and ideas.

1.  How did people’s reactions differ from 1994 to 2002?

Answers will vary, but may include: In 1994, people reacted with fear, confusion, and panic. In 2002, they reacted by trying to find a solution, helping others with prevention, medicine, and education.

2.  How did people’s medical and social ideas about the plague change from 1994 to 2002?

Answers will vary, but may include: In 1994, people believed that the plague was extremely deadly and contagious. They also believed that the plague could be prevented by avoiding contact with others. In 2002, people believed the plague could be cured with medicine and could be controlled by understanding and gathering information. In 1994, people believed that poor people were the source of the plague and that they should just help themselves and their families during the outbreak. In 2002, people believed that rats and fleas were the source of the plague and that people should work together to help the sick and dying.

3.  What mindsets and habits of character fostered these changes in ideas from 1994 to 2002?

Answers will vary, but may include: People in 2002 became effective learners. They took initiative, persevered, and collaborated to solve a problem that just a few years before had overwhelmed them and their country. They also showed that they were ethical people by treating the victims in the village with compassion, empathy, and respect. They gave the villagers medicine and taught them about prevention instead of shunning and scaring them as they had done in the 1994 breakout. Finally, those involved helped contribute to a better world by educating the public and stopping the spread of panic. These positive actions helped to create a healthy, equitable community in the village and throughout all of India.
