**Directions:** Reread the first section of the Model Podcast Script: “The Story of the Epidemic of 1994.” Answer the following questions to practice analyzing the interactions of individuals, events, and ideas.

1.  Who were some the individuals involved in the 1994 plague?

Answers will vary, but may include: general public, pharmacists, businessmen, officials in hospitals, doctors, city officials, children in slums, police.

2.  What actions did these individuals take in response to the event of the 1994 plague?

Answers will vary, but may include: they panicked and reacted in fear and confusion, elbowing and shoving their way onto trains and buses; took antibiotics and fled the city; prepared quarantine zones; closed schools, restaurants, parks, factories, businesses; stayed inside or wore medical masks or cloth over their faces; cordoned off the slums and ordered people to stay inside.

3.  Which medical ideas about the plague were most likely influencing the actions of these individuals? Underline all that apply.

4.  <u>The plague was extremely deadly.</u>

5.  <u>The plague was extremely contagious.</u>

6.  People had to leave the country to prevent the plague.

7.  People had to leave the country to find cures for the plague.

8.  <u>The plague could be cured with medicines such as antibiotics.</u>

9.  The plague could be controlled by understanding information.

10. <u>The plague could be prevented by avoiding contact with others.</u>

11. Which social ideas about the plague were most likely influencing the actions of these individuals? Underline all that apply.

12. Rich people could not contract the plague.

13. <u>Poor people were the original source of the plague.</u>

14. Rats and fleas were the original source of the plague.

15. Poor people should be responsible for caring for the rich during an outbreak.

16. Rich people should be responsible for caring for the poor during an outbreak.

17. <u>People should only take care of themselves and their families during an outbreak.</u>

18. People should work together to take care of all sick and dying during an outbreak.
