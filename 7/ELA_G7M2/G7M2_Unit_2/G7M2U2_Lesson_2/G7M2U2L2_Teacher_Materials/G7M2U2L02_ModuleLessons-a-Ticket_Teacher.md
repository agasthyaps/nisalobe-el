**Directions**: As you enter class, respond to the following prompts.

Use your background knowledge and prior experiences to define the following words. These words may be new to you, so this is a chance for you to work independently before reviewing with the class.

1.  argument:

An assertion or opinion and its supporting points, evidence, and reasoning

2.  main claim:

A statement of opinion that can be proven or disproven with evidence and reasoning

3.  point:

An important idea or reason used to support a claim

4.  evidence:

Information such as examples, data, facts and quotes that support an idea

5.  reasoning:

Explanations of why a claim is true, including explanations of how the evidence given proves the claim

How are the above words related? Again, this may be new for you, so write your best idea. Responses will be reviewed in class.

An argument is made up of a main claim and points, which are supported by evidence and reasoning.
