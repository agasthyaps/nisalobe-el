# Lesson 3: Analyze Setting, Characters, and Plot: *A Long Walk to Water*, Chapters 1–2
**Grade 7: Module 1: Unit 1: Lesson 3**

**Summary:** In this lesson, students work as a class to generate strategies to answer selected response questions. Then students read chapter 2 of *A Long Walk to Water*, noting unfamiliar vocabulary and the gist of the chapter. As a whole class, students analyze how the setting affects the character and plot d

**Focus Standards:** RL.7.3, RL.7.6, L.7.4, L.7.4a

**Learning Targets:**
- I can identify strategies to answer selected response questions.
- I can analyze how the setting shapes the characters and plot in chapters 1 and 2 of *A Long Walk to Water*. (RL.7.1, RL.7.3)
- I can explain how the author developed Salva’s and Nya’s points of view of life in chapter 2 of *A Long Walk to Water*. (RL.7.1, RL.7.6)

**Agenda:** Opening → Engage the Learner – L.7.4a (5 minutes) → Strategies to Answer Selected Response Questions (5 minutes) → Work Time → Read *A Long Walk to Water*, Chapter 2 (15 minutes) → Analyze Setting, Characters, and Plot – RL.7.3 (10 minutes)
