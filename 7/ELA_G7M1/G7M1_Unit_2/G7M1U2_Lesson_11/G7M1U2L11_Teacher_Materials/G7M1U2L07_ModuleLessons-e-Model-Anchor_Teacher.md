Criteria of an Effective Informative Essay  
Anchor Chart

W.7.2

(For Teacher Reference)

**Introduction**

- Introduction that catches the reader’s attention and gives context for the main people, places, or events in the essay

- Focus statement: States how the author uses or alters the history provided in the article “The ‘Lost Girls’ of Sudan”

**Proof Paragraph 1**

- States the first point

- Provides facts and evidence from the text(s) that support Point 1

- Elaboration: What do these facts and evidence show? Why?

- Restatement and/or reflection about how this point and evidence supports the focus statement

**Proof Paragraph 2**

- Transitions well from the first paragraph

- States the second point

- Provides facts and evidence from the text(s) that support Point 2

- Elaboration: What do these facts and evidence show? Why?

- Restatement and/or reflection about how this point and evidence supports the focus statement

**Conclusion**

- What? Restates the focus statement and evidence

- So what? Reflects on the larger meaning behind the focus statement and evidence
