Language Dive Guide: Compare and Contrast Model Essay, Conclusion

W.7.2f

(For Teacher Reference)

**Sentence**

**Although** the article and the novel focus on different parts of the journey, they discuss similar events. (from the fourth paragraph of the Compare and Contrast Model Essay)

**Rationale**

- Daily Learning Target and Focus Standards: The sentence helps students to address the Daily Learning Target (I can plan a conclusion that includes a strong reflection.) and W.7.2f by providing a model of an effective concluding statement.

- Guiding Question: The sentence helps students respond to the guiding question (Who are the Lost Children of Sudan, and what is their story?) by comparing two texts written about the Lost Children of Sudan.

- Language Structure: This sentence is compelling because it uses the subordinating conjunction **although** with the purpose of contrasting two ideas within the sentence. Invite students to discuss each chunk briefly, but encourage extended conversation and practice with the focus structure: **although**.

- Practice and Transfer: After discussing the focus structure, students practice using it to discuss something they like and dislike in the novel *A Long Walk to Water*. Students transfer their understanding of the meaning and structure of this sentence when writing the conclusion paragraph for their compare and contrast essay during this lesson, and when writing their conclusion paragraph during the End of Unit 2 Assessment.
