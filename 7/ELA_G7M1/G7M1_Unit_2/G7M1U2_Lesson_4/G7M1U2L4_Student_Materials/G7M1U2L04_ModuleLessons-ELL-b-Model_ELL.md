The article “One Day I Had to Run” is about John Deng Langbany, a Lost Boy of Sudan. It tells the story of his long and dangerous journey to escape the civil war in his country. Langbany first describes “running with thousands of other young children in a very hard journey” across the desert to Ethiopia. The children faced many dangers. They were threatened by wild animals and soldiers along the way. Then Langbany describes his life in the Kakuma refugee camp. There, “the native people treated us badly because they didn’t know us.” Langbany also writes about the dangers in crossing the Gilo River. Finally, Langbany describes his adventure coming to the United States to live. This article shows how long and dangerous Langbany’s journey was away from civil war in his homeland.
