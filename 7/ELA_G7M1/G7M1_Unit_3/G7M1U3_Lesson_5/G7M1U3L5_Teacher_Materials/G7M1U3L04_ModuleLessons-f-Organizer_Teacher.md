Narrative Writing Plan Graphic Organizer

W.7.3

(Example for Teacher Reference)

Use this organizer to keep track of the material needed for your narrative.

**Part I: Think about Purpose**

**Task (what I’m writing)**

A narrative, or a story, about a Lost Boy or Girl of Sudan. The story will be in the form of an ebook, which can be shared on computers or other devices, and also is meant to be read aloud.

**Purpose (why I am writing it)**

I am writing to share the story of the Lost Boys or Girls of Sudan with others, and in particular to talk about the habits of character the Lost Boys or Girls relied on to survive their difficult journey.

**Audience (who I am writing it for)**

This ebook is for elementary school students.

**Part III: Plan a Plot**

Responses will vary. (Examples from *Brothers in Hope* by Mary Luana Williams)

**Background**

Garang had a good life living with his family and tending livestock on his farm in southern Sudan.

**Event or Action**

War breaks out, and Garang is separated from his family.

**Event or Action**

Garang finds other Lost Boys and starts his journey to Ethiopia.

**Event or Action**

Garang takes care of Chuti along the journey and arrives in Ethiopia.

**Climax**

Garang is forced to leave Ethiopia and reunites with Chuti and Tom at the camp in Kenya.

**Reflection**

When Tom leaves camp, Garang becomes a leader, organizing activities and taking care of others.

**Ending**

Tom returns to camp and explains that he’s been spreading the Lost Boys’ story and Garang and the others now have a chance to go to America.

**Sources**

Responses will vary.
