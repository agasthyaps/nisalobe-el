**Sentence**

Now she can see **blue sky beyond those dark clouds.** (from page 22 of *Nasreen‚Äôs Secret School* by Jeanette Winter)

**Rationale**

- Daily Learning Target and Focus Standards: The sentence helps students to address the Daily Learning Target (I can determine the meaning and impact of figurative language.), RL.7.4 and L.7.5 by providing an example of figurative language to describe a feeling of hope. The sentence also helps students to address W.7.3e by providing an example of an effective conclusion that reflects on the narrated experiences and events in the story.

- Guiding Question: The sentence helps students respond to the guiding question (What are the habits of character the Lost Children used to survive?) by sharing a reflection of a character who demonstrates perseverance amidst hardship, similar to the Lost Children of Sudan.

- Language Structure: This sentence is compelling because it uses figurative language for the purpose of providing an example of an effective narrative conclusion. Invite students to discuss each chunk briefly, but encourage extended conversation and practice with the focus structure: **blue sky beyond those dark clouds.**

- Practice and Transfer: After discussing the focus structure, students practice using it to discuss something in their lives. Students transfer their understanding of the meaning and structure of this sentence when planning for their narratives in this lesson, and when writing a reflection and ending for their narrative during the End of Unit 3 Assessment.
