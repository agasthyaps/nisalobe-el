Verb Mood Organizer

L.8.1c, L.8.3a

(Answers for Teacher Reference)

**Directions:** Match the sentences with the mood of the verb.

<u>Did</u> you <u>finish</u> your homework?

If I finished my homework, then I <u>would be able to</u> relax.

<u>Finish</u> your homework!

<u>Will</u> you <u>finish</u> your homework tonight?

I demanded you <u>finish</u> your homework yesterday.

I <u>have finished</u> my homework.

I <u>could finish</u> my homework right after school if I didn’t have basketball practice.

<u>Are</u> you <u>finishing</u> your homework right now?

I suggest that you <u>finish</u> your homework tonight.

It is essential that I <u>finish</u> my homework tomorrow.

I <u>finished</u> my homework last night.

I <u>had finished</u> my homework by 5 p.m. last night.

You <u>might finish</u> your homework quickly at the library.

I <u>will finish</u> my homework tonight, and I <u>will review</u> it tomorrow.

<td><p><strong>Imperative Mood</strong></p>
<p>Expresses commands or requests and indicates that the speaker desires for the action expressed in the sentence to take place. In imperative sentences, the subject is <strong>you</strong>, but it is not stated in the sentence.</p>
<p>(base form of verb + [object] + !)</p></td>

<tr class="even">
<td><p><strong>Conditional Mood</strong></p>
<p>Expresses possibility or uncertainty, based on something else happening first, through use of words like <strong>might</strong>, <strong>could</strong>, and <strong>would</strong> before the base form of the verb.</p>
<p>(subject + <strong>would</strong> + base form of verb + [object])</p></td>

<p><strong>You <u>might finish</u> your homework quickly at the library.</strong></p></td>

<td><p><strong>Subjunctive Mood</strong></p>
<p>Expresses imaginary or possible outcomes, suggestions, demands, necessity, and wishes. The subjunctive is formed using the base form of a verb (for example: <strong>be</strong>, <strong>go</strong>, <strong>have</strong>, <strong>finish</strong>) after the subject in a <strong>that</strong> clause.</p>
<p>(subject + verb [<strong>that</strong>] + subject + base form of verb + object)</p></td>
