<td colspan="2"><p>Gist Statement:</p>
<p><strong>Elie and his family arrive at a concentration camp, and Elie is afraid and losing hope.</strong></p></td>

<tr class="even">
<td><p>Key Plot Elements</p>
<p><strong>Elie stands in a line with his father, mother, and sister at a concentration camp.</strong></p>
<p><strong>The soldiers start to lead Elie’s group toward the fiery pit.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Men begin to pray and Elie’s father weeps, but Elie is angry at God for allowing people to die this way.</strong></p>
<p><strong>He contemplates running to the barbed wire to die quickly, rather than let the Nazis burn him alive.</strong></p>
<p><strong>As Elie and his father are almost at the pit of flames and Elie prepares to head to the barbed wire, his group is directed past the flames to barracks and are not killed by the soldiers.</strong></p></td>
<td><p>Character and Analysis</p>
<p>How would you describe Elie’s character?</p>
<p><strong>afraid, despairing</strong></p>
<p>What in the text makes you think so?</p>
<p><strong>Elie thinks about committing suicide because he is afraid to die in the fiery pit.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Elie is angry with God for letting people die this way and doubts his Jewish faith as people pray around him.</strong></p></td>
</tr>

<td><p>Theme(s)</p>

<td><p>Evidence from Text to Support Theme(s)</p>
<p><strong>“Never shall I forget those flames that consumed my faith forever” (34).</strong></p>
<p><strong>“I gathered all that remained of my strength in order to break rank and throw myself onto the barbed wire. Deep down, I was saying good-bye to my father, to the whole universe . . .” (33–34)</strong></p></td>
