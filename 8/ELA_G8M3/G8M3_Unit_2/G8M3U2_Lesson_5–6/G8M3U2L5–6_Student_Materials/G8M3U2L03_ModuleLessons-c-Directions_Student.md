Literary Analysis Informative Essay Directions

W.8.2

**Name:** **Date:**

**Write a Literary Analysis Informative Essay**

Throughout this unit, you will work independently to plan an essay to answer the prompt below:

**Prompt:** Write an essay in which you compare the theme of *Maus I* and “Often a Minute,” and analyze how the differing structures contribute to that theme.

REMEMBER: A well-written, informative essay

- clearly states a focus and previews what is to follow,

- stays focused throughout the essay,

- uses relevant, well-chosen facts, definitions, concrete details, quotations, or other information and examples to develop your topic and explain your thinking,

- groups information logically, in a way that makes your thinking clear to the reader,

- follows the rules of writing (spelling, punctuation, and grammar),

- uses appropriate and varied transitions to create cohesion and clarify the relationships among ideas and concepts,

- uses precise language and domain-specific vocabulary to inform about or explain the topic,

- establishes and maintains a formal style, and

- provides a concluding statement or section that follows from and supports the information or explanation presented.

Use the following documents to assist you in planning and writing your essay:

- *Maus I* and “Often a Minute”

- Informative Writing Plan graphic organizer

- Model Literary Analysis Essay: Relationship of Structure to Meaning

- Vocabulary log

- Online or print dictionaries (including ELL and home language dictionaries)
