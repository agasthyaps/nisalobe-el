**Text Structures:** Writers use varying structures to organize the content in a text or piece of writing, depending on their purpose. The structure used determines how the text is organized.

<tr class="even">
<td><p><strong>Narrative Plot Structure:</strong> A classic narrative structure often includes a beginning, rising action, climax, falling action, and a conclusion.</p>
<p><strong>Chapters:</strong> Main divisions of a book, typically with a number or title.</p>
<p><strong>Flashbacks:</strong> An interruption in the normal sequence of events in a literary narrative; it draws attention to something that happened at an earlier time.</p>
<p><strong>Sequential/Chronological Structure:</strong> The author provides the reader with chronological events.</p></td>
<td><p><strong>Stanzas:</strong> A group of lines within a poem, often four or more lines, sometimes having a fixed length, meter, or rhyming sequence.</p>
<p><strong>Verse:</strong> A single line of a poem, or a whole stanza (such as in a song).</p>
<p><strong>Chorus:</strong> A stanza in a poem or song that is repeated over the course of the song.</p>
<p><strong>Repetition:</strong> When authors use a word or phrase more than once, to create a pattern or for the purpose of emphasis.</p></td>
