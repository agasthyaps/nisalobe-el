**Focus Question:** Write an essay in which you compare and contrast the structures of *Maus I* and “Often a Minute,” and analyze how the differing structures of each text contribute to their meaning.

Focus

What is the main idea of your piece?

Same topic, nuanced theme. Each uses a different structure to convey the theme.

Introduction

How will you catch the reader’s attention?

- Throughout history, humans encountered adversity, large or small ways.

- Holocaust was one of most horrible cases of persecution and oppression in world history.

What context do you need to give to your reader?

- strong will to survive in many victims and survivors

- Two texts that we have read, *Maus I* and “Often a Minute,” explore human will to survive, perseverance in the face of terrible oppression.

Write your focus statement.

Two texts that we have read, *Maus I* and “Often a Minute,” explore the topic of the human spirit of perseverance, a will to live in the face of terrible oppression. Each text comes to a nuanced theme about this same topic. Each text also uses a different structure to convey that theme to the reader.

What are the main points you will be making to support this focus?

1\. Each text comes to a nuanced theme about this same topic.

2\. Each text also uses a different structure to convey that theme to the reader.

Proof Paragraphs for Point 1

What is the first point that supports your focus statement?

Each text comes to a nuanced theme about this same topic.

Details/Evidence for Text 1: ***Maus I***

What evidence from the text supports this point?

- has to send son away, son dies

- Wife does not want to go on living.

- Vladek refuses to die:

- “To die, it’s easy . . . but you have to struggle for life,” and he explains how they must move forward together (Spiegelman 122).

How does this evidence support this point?

- Theme: There is a strong human will to keep oneself and one’s family alive, despite tragedy and oppression.

Details/Evidence for Text 2: **“Often a Minute”**

What evidence from the text supports this point?

- frightened, has heard of deep persecution of Jewish people in many European countries

- ends with “Let’s not despair!” “This indestructible nation shall stand just.”  
  (Klein stanzas 5–6)

How does this evidence support this point?

- does not focus on survival of herself and family

- instead conveys a broader theme: will for an entire nation of people to persevere and survive

Proof Paragraphs for Point 2

What is the next point that supports your focus statement?

Each text also uses a different structure to convey that theme to the reader.

Details/Evidence for Text 1: ***Maus I***

What evidence from the text supports this point?

- uses largely chronological structure to tell Vladek’s story

- over and over, Vladek faced with unimaginable decisions

- repeatedly centers his life, and the life of family, as basis for all his choices

- example: Vladek was drafted into the army but was able to get out. He journeyed very far, through dangerous conditions, to find his way back to his family (Spiegelman  
  47–65).

How does this evidence support this point?

- The chronological plot structure emphasizes the many challenges and choices Vladek has to keep facing.

- highlights theme of the human will to survive and to protect one’s family

Details/Evidence for Text 2: **“Often a Minute”**

What evidence from the text supports this point?

- divided into stanzas

- Stanzas build to show shift in Klein’s thoughts and tone.

- In the first four stanzas, Klein reflects on current state of Jewish oppression in Europe. Tone is fearful, confused, and angry. She asks, “Why are we uselessly persecuted? / Why is our fate so desolate?” (stanza 4)

- In stanza 5, Klein’s thoughts and tone begin to shift. Becomes determined to survive, charges the Jewish people to maintain faith and solidarity.

- In stanza 6, Klein writes, “The Jewish spring will be arriving!”

How does this evidence support this point?

- structure shows shift in thought and tone

- goes from fear to determination for the Jewish people to survive

- develops the theme of human will for nation to survive, persevere

Conclusion

Restate your focus statement from the introduction.

- Two texts reveal similar topic of human survival and persistence in the face of oppression.

- However, each text develops a nuance to this theme. *Maus I* focuses on the survival of self and family. “Often a Minute” expresses the will of an entire people to stand in solidarity and endure.

- Texts use differing structures to support and convey each theme.

What are your further reflections on this topic?

- intentional use of structure for message important way of making literature memorable for readers

- Structure helps each effectively convey powerful messages about human endurance.
