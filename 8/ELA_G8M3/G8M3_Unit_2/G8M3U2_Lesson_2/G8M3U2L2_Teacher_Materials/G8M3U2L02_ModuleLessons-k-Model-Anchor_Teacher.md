<td><p>How is the theme of “Often a Minute” similar to or different from that of <em>Maus I</em>?</p>
<p>The theme of “Often a Minute” is that the human will to survive and persevere can extend to an entire nation of people who stand together. This is <u>similar</u> to a theme in <em>Maus I</em>, that there is a strong human will to survive and preserve in the face of oppression.</p></td>
<td><p>How is the structure Klein uses in “Often a Minute” different or similar to the structure Art Spiegelman uses to contribute to the meaning of <em>Maus I</em>?</p>
<p>Spiegelman uses the flashbacks of his father’s chronological recounting of his life to portray this theme. <u>In contrast</u>, Klein uses stanzas that represent a shift in thought and tone to convey this theme.</p></td>
