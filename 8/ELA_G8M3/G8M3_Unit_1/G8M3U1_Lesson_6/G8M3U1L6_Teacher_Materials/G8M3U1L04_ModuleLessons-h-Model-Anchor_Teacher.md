<p><strong>Vladek is a hard worker and is ambitious.</strong></p>

<tr class="odd">
<td colspan="2">Chapter 3 Gist: <strong>Vladek is drafted in the war and becomes a Nazi prisoner of war.</strong></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><p>Key Plot Elements</p>
<p><strong>Vladek’s father starves him to prevent him from meeting weight requirements for the draft. It works, but he is eventually drafted in the war against the Germans.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Nazis capture Vladek. He starves and freezes at a prisoner camp.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Vladek volunteers to work for a German company to live in better conditions.</strong></p>
<p><strong>After some months pass, Vladek is able to disguise himself as a Polish man and get back to his family.</strong></p></td>
<td><p>Character Development and Analysis</p>
<p><strong>Vladek shows agency, bathing in cold water, doing gymnastics, praying—now, agency shows up as a deep will to survive.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Volunteering for the German labor camp also shows strong will to survive—he has made a choice when given one to help himself survive.</strong></p>
<p><strong>He will do anything to get back to his family—this shows how much he cares about them, also how resourceful he is in figuring out how to do it—and agency here (acting in his own interest) shows up as a deep will to survive and to get back to his family.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Perseverance—Vladek does not give up.</strong></p></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td colspan="2"><p>Emerging Themes</p>
<p>Perseverance, agency, the will to survive, and family ties:</p>
<p><strong>•</strong> <strong>Humans have an amazing ability to persevere through extremely challenging events and oppression.</strong></p>
<p><strong>•</strong> <strong>Humans care about having their family stay alive and stay together.</strong></p>
<p>Remembering:</p>
<p><strong>•</strong> <strong>Humans tell stories of a painful past so that others can learn about history.</strong></p></td>

</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><p>Key Plot Elements</p>
<p><strong>Vladek finds different schemes to earn money and keep the Nazis from taking him to a camp.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Vladek uses money, his friendships, and connections to remain alive and protect his family.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Vladek considers sending his son Richieu to live with strangers to keep him alive.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Jews are hanged in the street for giving cloth to Jews without coupons.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Elderly people are sent to death camps.</strong></p>
<p><strong>The Jewish people are terrified, and Vladek is trying to stay alive.</strong></p></td>
<td><p>Character Development and Analysis</p>
<p><strong>Vladek is resourceful and will do anything to keep his family alive and safe.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Vladek wants to keep his family together but also wants to do what is best to keep his son alive.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Anja wants to keep her family together at all costs.</strong></p></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td colspan="2"><p>Emerging Themes</p>
<p>Perseverance, agency, persistence, and family ties:</p>
<p><strong>•</strong> <strong>Humans are faced with difficult decisions when their lives are at risk.</strong></p>
<p><strong>•</strong> <strong>Humans make difficult choices in order to persevere and try to keep themselves and their families alive.</strong></p></td>

<tr class="odd">
<td colspan="2">Chapter 5 Gist: <strong>Vladek uses money and connections to hide from the Gestapo and stay alive.</strong></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><p>Key Plot Elements</p>
<p><strong>Vladek discovers a comic Art created years ago about his mother’s suicide.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Vladek gets emotional about those memories, but continues to tell Art his Holocaust stories.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Vladek uses jewelry he saved to pay influential people to hide him and Anja from the Gestapo and get him jobs to keep him alive.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Thousands and thousands of Jews are being sent to Auschwitz to be killed and the ghettos of Poland are nearly empty.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Vladek sends his first son, Richieu, to a safe place with a friend, but the Gestapo raid their ghetto.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Caregiver poisons herself, Richieu, and the other children in her care so they won’t have to die in the gas chambers at Auschwitz.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Vladek and Anja keep losing friends and family, but they find a bunker to hide in as they starve with other Jewish friends.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Vladek shows Art the valuables he hid during the Holocaust and managed to retrieve when the war was over. He tells Art he wants him to have his valuables after he dies.</strong></p></td>
<td><p>Character Development and Analysis</p>
<p><strong>Vladek is deeply sad and despairing about the death of his late wife, Anja—he cared for his family and worked very hard to keep them alive, but in the end lost a son and a wife.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Anja was unable to bear the pain of what she experienced during the Holocaust.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Vladek believes it is important to share his story of the Holocaust with his son, even though it pains him to remember.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Vladek showed agency in making multiple difficult choices to try to keep himself and his family alive.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Richieu’s caregiver chose her own death rather than waiting for the Nazis.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Vladek and Anja persevere through extremely harsh conditions while going into hiding.</strong></p></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td colspan="2"><p>Emerging Themes</p>
<p>Perseverance, agency, persistence, and family ties:</p>
<p><strong>•</strong> <strong>Humans make difficult choices in order to try to keep themselves and their families alive.</strong></p>
<p><strong>•</strong> <strong>There is a deep human will to survive and preserve.</strong></p>
<p>Remembering:</p>
<p><strong>•</strong> <strong>Reflecting on the past can be painful.</strong></p>
<p><strong>•</strong> <strong>The effects of the Holocaust are complicated, painful, and long lasting.</strong></p>
<p><strong>•</strong> <strong>Humans tell stories of a painful past so that others can learn about history and learn not to repeat it.</strong></p></td>
</tr>

<td colspan="2">Chapter 6 Gist: <strong>Vladek pays Polish friends to hide him and Anja, but the Gestapo capture them and take them to Auschwitz.</strong></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><p>Key Plot Elements</p>
<p><strong>Vladek pays a Polish woman to hide in her barn, then another Polish woman to hide in her cellar.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Vladek and Anja risk their lives while these Polish women risk their own safety by hiding Jews in their home.</strong></p>
<p><strong>In the cellar, Vladek and Anja are starving, cold, and living amongst rats.</strong></p>
<p><strong>They meet smugglers who take Jews to Hungary to escape the war.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Vladek pays them to smuggle them over to Hungary, but the smugglers turn them in to the Gestapo instead.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Vladek and Anja are taken to Auschwitz.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Art asks Vladek about his mother’s diaries that she wrote throughout the war and after. Vladek reveals that he burned all of her books because he was depressed over losing her. Art is furious with his father.</strong></p></td>
<td><p>Character Development and Analysis</p>
<p><strong>Some people risked their lives to protect Jews during the Holocaust.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Vladek and Anja made many difficult choices in the face of oppression in order to stay alive and stay together.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Vladek and Anja survived terrible conditions.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Anja’s spirit was incredibly damaged by the terrifying events she experienced during the Holocaust.</strong></p>
<p><strong>It is very difficult for Valdek to remember his painful past and the memory of Anja.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Art feels like a part of his mother was destroyed when Vladek destroyed her diaries.</strong></p></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td colspan="2"><p>Most Prominent Themes</p>
<ol type="1">
<li><p><strong>There is a deep human will to persevere, to keep oneself and one’s family alive, in the face of oppression.</strong></p></li>
<li><p><strong>It is important to remember a painful past in order to educate future generations.</strong></p></li>
</ol></td>
