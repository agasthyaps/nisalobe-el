</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td>Key Details</td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><p><strong>Lutz began issuing protective passports to allow Budapest’s Jews to escape Nazi-occupied Hungary.</strong></p>
<p><strong>He convinced the Germans to allow him to issue 8,000 diplomatic letters of protection.</strong></p>
<p><strong>He duplicated the letters for entire families, saving approximately 62,000 people, when Nazis stopped deporting Jews and brutally murdered them in Hungary.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Lutz organized safe houses across the country to protect Jewish people.</strong></p>
<p><strong>After the war, Lutz returned to Switzerland, but no one acknowledged his bravery.</strong></p></td>

<td>Central Idea</td>

<tr class="even">
<td>Supporting Evidence</td>
</tr>
