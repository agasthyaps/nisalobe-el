# Lesson 2: Voices of Upstanders: Carl Lutz
**Grade 8: Module 3: Unit 3: Lesson 2**

**Summary:** Students read an informational text about a Holocaust upstander, consider characteristics that make this person an upstander, and write a reflection. Students also participate in a mini lesson on punctuation.

**Focus Standards:** RI.8.1, RI.8.2, W.8.4, L.8.1b, L.8.1d, L.8.2a, L.8.2b

**Learning Targets:**
- I can determine a central idea and analyze its development in the article “The Forgotten Swiss Diplomat Who Rescued Thousands from Holocaust.” (RI.8.2)
- I can write a text reflection about “The Forgotten Swiss Diplomat Who Rescued Thousands from Holocaust.” (RI.8.1, RI.8.2)
- I can use commas, dashes, and ellipses to indicate a pause, break, or omission. (L.8.2a, L.8.2b)

**Agenda:** Opening → Engage the Learner – L.8.1d (5 minutes) → Work Time → Read “The Forgotten Swiss Diplomat Who Rescued Thousands from Holocaust,” and Identify Central Idea – RI.8.2 (15 minutes) → Write a Text Reflection – RI.8.1 (15 minutes) → Closing and Assessment
