**Characteristics of Compare and Contrast Essays**

**Introductory Paragraph**

- Introduction that catches the reader’s attention and gives context

- Focus statement: answers the prompt clearly, naming the similarities and differences (for example: differences and similarities between the monster in the folklore and the way the author modernized it)

**Proof Paragraph 1**

- States the similarities (between the monster in the folklore and the way the author modernized it)

- Provides facts and evidence from the text(s) that support point 1

- Elaboration: What do these facts and evidence show? Why?

- Restatement and/or reflection about how this shows the similarities (between the monster in the folklore and the way the author modernized it)

**Proof Paragraph 2**

- Transitions well from the first paragraph

- States the difference (between the monster in the folklore and the way the author modernized it)

- Provides facts and evidence from the text(s) that support point 2

- Elaboration: What do these facts and evidence show? Why?

- Restatement and/or reflection about how this shows the differences (between the monster in the folklore and the way the author modernized it)

**Conclusion Paragraph**

- What?: Restates the focus statement and reasons

- So what?: Reflects why the author modernized the monster and what theme this conveys
