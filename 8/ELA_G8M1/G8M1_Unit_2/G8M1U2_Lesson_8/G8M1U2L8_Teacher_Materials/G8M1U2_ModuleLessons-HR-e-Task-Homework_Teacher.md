11. What is Juanita’s character profile? Use evidence from the book to support your answer. (RL.8.1, W.8.3a)

Juanita is an independent thinker. She challenges Odilia, but she is also very caring.  
When the twins are obsessed with being on television for finding the dead man,  
Juanita tells the girls to stop focusing on themselves, “‘This isn’t a movie set, and  
you’re not starlets yet . . . This is about him, not us’” (9).

12. What is Velia and Delia’s character profile? Use evidence from the book to support your answer. (RL.8.1, W.8.3a)

The twins, Velia and Delia, are close. They think quickly to solve problems. For example, when the girls are attacked by the lechuzas, Velia and Delia run out of the barn. Odilia thinks they are abandoning them, but then Odilia says “Just when I thought all was lost, Velia and Delia burst through the barn doors wielding . . . metal baseball bats” (205).  
They use the bats to fight the lechuzas off of their sisters.
