Character and Setting: “The Peuchen”

W.8.3a, W.8.4

**Name:** **Date:**

**Character Name:** Peuchen

**Where in the book does this fit?** Between chapters 12 and 13.

**What are elements of the original monster in the modernization?** Changes into flying snake. Eyes hypnotize. Female Peuchen describes how she drank the blood of a sheep to not be a threat to the girls.

**What does it look like when it first appears in the modernization?** Looks like La Llorona, but makes hissing sounds (because it is really a snake).

**How does it behave when it first appears in the modernization?** Friendly and helpful like La Llorona. Gives Odilia advice to leave.

**How is the monster modernized?** Two Peuchens—brother and sister. Sister helps the girls and fights her brother who wants to kill them.

**Why this modernization?** To continue to illustrate the theme from the novel that judging by appearances can be deceiving.

**Where does the narrative take place? When? What does it look like?** Off the track in and around caves in a cliff wall. Never-ending hills and cliffs, occasional tree, sandy desert, scree field, in the dark of night.
