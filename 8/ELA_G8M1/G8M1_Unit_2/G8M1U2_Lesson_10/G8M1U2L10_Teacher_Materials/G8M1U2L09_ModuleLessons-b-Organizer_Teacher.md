<th><p><strong>Rising Action 1</strong></p>
<p>After you introduce the characters and setting, how do the characters sense danger?</p>
<p>How do the characters know something is wrong?</p>

<td><p><strong>Rising Action 2</strong></p>
<p>What is the next incident that builds more tension in the story?</p>
<p>How do the characters react to the danger?</p>
<p>incident: an event or occurrence</p>
<p>tension: an anxious feeling</p></td>
<td><strong>For a moment, the figure looks and sounds like La Llorona, and Odilia is relieved. But she is surprised by the hissing noise the figure makes, and when Juanita screams she realized the snake-like creature is a peuchen!</strong></td>

<tr class="even">
<td><p><strong>Rising Action 3</strong></p>
<p>What happens right before the big climax?</p>
<p>How do the characters and/or the audience know something dangerous is about to happen?</p>
<p>climax: the most intense or exciting part</p></td>
<td><strong>Even though the peuchen says she wants to help the sisters and will not harm them, she warns that they are in great danger.</strong></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><p><strong>Climax</strong></p>
<p>What is the turning point in the narrative?</p>
<p>What is the most intense or important event in the narrative?</p>
<p>turning point: a time or event when change occurs</p></td>
<td><strong>The first peuchen transforms into her full snake form and is joined by a second, bigger peuchen, her brother. They fight as the brother wants to attack the sisters and feed on them while his sister wants to fend him off. This is important because the sisters are in grave danger during this fight.</strong></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><p><strong>Falling Action</strong></p>
<p>What happens right after the climax?</p>
<p>What are the consequences or results of the climax?</p></td>
<td><strong>The sisters use the distraction as a chance to try to escape and find another passage out of the cave, leading to another exit.</strong></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><p><strong>Resolution</strong></p>
<p>How is the major conflict solved?</p>
<p>How do the characters work out their problem(s)?</p>
<p>How does your narrative end?</p>
<p>conflict: a problem or challenge</p>
<p><strong>Quick sketch of girls in a cave</strong></p></td>
<td><strong>Girls escape from monsters through a cave and out into the darkness. Ends with Odilia reflecting on what just happened with the monsters.</strong></td>
