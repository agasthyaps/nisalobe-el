11. **Part A**

On pages 76–77, what happens to the narrator that the reader knows about, but the other sisters don’t? (RL.8.1, RL.8.6)

Sample student response: Odilia sees La Llorona standing in a field and knows she needs to get back to her sisters because something isn’t right.

**Part B**

Why might the author have chosen to give the reader this perspective? What effect does it create? (RL.8.6)

Sample student response: It creates suspense because La Llorona has shown herself to Odilia for a reason, and seeing her leaves Odilia with the feeling she needs to return to her sisters. Like Odilia, we are worried about what has happened to her sisters.
