Exit Ticket: Analyze Point of View:  
*Summer of the Mariposas*, Chapter 2 Excerpt

RL.8.1, RL.8.6

(Answers for Teacher Reference)

Read this excerpt from page 43, then answer the following questions:

“It wasn’t at all how I had planned our night to end, but I figured all hope wasn’t lost. It was just easier to let my sisters think they’d won.”

1.  From reading this excerpt, what do you, the reader, know that Odilia’s sisters don’t know? (RL.8.6)

2.  Odilia is very upset with them for following the plan to move the body.

3.  Odilia is pleased with them for following the plan to move the body.

4.  <u>Odilia intends to prevent their plan from moving forward.</u>

5.  Odilia doesn’t know how to drive after all.

6.  What effect does this create? (RL.8.6)

(All of the underlined student responses are appropriate.)

7.  <u>excitement</u>

8.  humor

9.  <u>suspense</u>

10. <u>relief</u>

11. How does the author create this effect? (RL.8.1, RL.8.6)

Sample student response: By knowing the narrator’s thoughts and feelings, we can feel what the narrator is feeling. We know Odilia plans to prevent the plan from moving forward, so the reader might feel excitement or suspense about how she is going to stop them, or relief that she is going to prevent them from putting themselves in danger and getting into trouble.
