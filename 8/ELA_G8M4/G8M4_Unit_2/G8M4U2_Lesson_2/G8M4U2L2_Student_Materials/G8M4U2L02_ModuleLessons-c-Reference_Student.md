Synopsis: *Farewell to Manzanar*, Chapter 13

**Name:** **Date:**

**Chapter 13 “Outings, Explorations”**

- Jeanne gradually starts to feel better about life now that the family has moved to Block 28.

- Jeanne and her brothers and sisters attend school in Manzanar. Jeanne joins a Glee Club and spends the end of the school day signing. After school and on weekends, Jeanne participates in a recreation program, doing arts and crafts and going on nature hikes.

- Jeanne and other children go on an overnight camping trip away from camp.

- Jeanne joins the baton club at school, and it becomes her favorite hobby.

- Jeanne also takes traditional Japanese dance lessons. Some of the girls in Jeanne’s class tell her to put hair products on her face and skin products in her hair and that she should wear her clothes inside out. Jeanne believes them and follows their advice because she wants them to accept her, but Mama tells her that the girls were saying these things to be mean to her.

- Jeanne also takes ballet lessons, but she doesn’t enjoy them.

- Jeanne continues her study of Catholicism, and she decides she wants to be baptized. When she shares this with Papa, he refuses to let her do so. Jeanne is angry and disappointed at his decision.
