**Write a Practice Argument Essay**

Throughout this unit, you will work with a partner to plan and write an essay that relates to the background and task explained below.

**Background and Task**

One significant idea in the text *Farewell to Manzanar* is that Papa feels deeply conflicted loyalties, both to Japan and to America. This idea is significant because it contributes to a deeper understanding of the text overall. You have uncovered this significant idea through a close analysis of scenes, connections, and distinctions across the text.

In bringing *Farewell to Manzanar* from its pages to the big screen, the filmmakers make a variety of choices. These choices may, or may not, lead to an effective presentation of significant ideas from the text.

With a partner, you will write an argument essay to state and support your answer to the following question: How effectively does the film *Farewell to Manzanar* convey the significant idea that Papa feels deeply conflicted loyalties, both to Japan and to America?

You will create a plan for the practice essay during Lesson 11, and have time to write the essay in pairs during Lessons 12â€“16.

**REMEMBER: A well-written argument essay**

- introduces and clearly states a main claim;

- provides logically ordered points/reasons that are supported by evidence;

- links claim and reasons using words and phrases;

- identifies a counterclaim and distinguishes this from the main claim;

- provides a conclusion that restates and reflects on the argument presented; and

- follows the rules of writing (spelling, punctuation, and grammar).
