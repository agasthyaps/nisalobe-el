**Directions:** Read Section 8 (pages 40–44) of *The Omnivore’s Dilemma*, then answer the questions about language below.

1.  Why did the writer use the word **money** in this sentence instead of using a synonym with a different connotation, such as **cash**? (L.8.5c)

“To get the corn flowing and keep it flowing, agribusiness depends on government regulation and taxpayer money” (40).

2.  **Money** has a less formal connotation, so it makes sense to use this word when talking about the government.

3.  <u>**Money** has a more formal connotation, so it makes sense to use this word when talking about the government.</u>

4.  **Money** connotes currency used to purchase crops, so it makes sense to use this word when talking about farming.

5.  **Money** connotes currency used by the government, so it makes sense to use this word when talking about government spending.

6.  In the sentence below, how does the word **forests** help us to better understand **the timber industry**? (L.8.5b)

“One idea was to spray it on forests to help out the timber industry” (41).

7.  The word **forests** tell us that **the timber industry** is an industry that produces nitrogen.

8.  The word **forests** tell us that **the timber industry** is an industry that produces corn.

9.  <u>The word **forests** tell us that **the timber industry** is an industry that produces trees.</u>

10. The word **forests** tell us that **the timber industry** is an industry that produces GMOs.

11. What is the difference between the connotations of the synonyms **crop** and **plants** in these sentences? (L.8.5c)

“Chemical fertilizer was needed to grow hybrid corn because it is a very hungry crop” (42).

“The richest acre of Iowa soil could never feed thirty thousand hungry corn plants year after year without added fertilizer” (42).

12. <u>**Crops** connotes a product to be sold, while **plants** connotes the natural organism.</u>

13. **Plants** connotes a product to be sold, while **crops** connotes the natural organism.

14. **Crops** has a warmer more personal connotation, while **plants** has a formal, impersonal connotation.

15. **Crops** has a stronger, serious connotation, while **plants** has a light and silly connotation.
