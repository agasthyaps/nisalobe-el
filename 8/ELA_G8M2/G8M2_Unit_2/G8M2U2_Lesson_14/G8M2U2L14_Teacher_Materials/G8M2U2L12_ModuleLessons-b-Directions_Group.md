Desktop Teaching Activity Directions

RI.8.1, SL.8.4, SL.8.5, L.8.6

**Preparation:**

In Lessons 7–11, you planned and drafted an informative essay about your independent research case study. In your essay, you explained ways in which your case study both increases and decreases access to healthy food.

For your end of unit assessment, you will pretend to be teachers and use your lesson plan to teach a small group of students about your research findings.

**Directions:**

- You will be split into three groups by your case study topic: Group 1, Group 2, and Group 3.

- Group 1 students will be assigned to stations and will set up their presentation materials, including their lesson plan, informative essay (optional), exit tickets, and multimedia/visual display.

- These students will be the “teachers” of Round 1.

- The rest of the class will be split into pairs or triads and be assigned to a station.

- These students will be the “students” of Round 1.

- “Teachers” will have 5 minutes to present their research findings.

- After 5 minutes pass, “students” will have 2 minutes to ask the “teachers” questions and 2 minutes to copy the exit ticket question and answer the question on their End of Unit 2 Assessment, Part I: Desktop Teaching Activity Lesson Plan.

- “Teachers” will gather their materials and hand them in.

- Group 2 “teachers” will be assigned stations and will set up their presentation materials.

- Repeat the steps above for Round 2.

- Once Round 2 is complete, Group 3 “teachers” will have a few minutes to set up their presentation materials.

- Repeat the steps above for Round 3.
