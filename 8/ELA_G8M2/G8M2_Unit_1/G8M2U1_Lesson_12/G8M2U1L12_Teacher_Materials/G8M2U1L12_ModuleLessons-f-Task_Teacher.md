Analyze Different Mediums:  
Industrial Organic Farming

RI.8.7, RI.8.9

(Answers for Teacher Reference)

**Directions:** Work with your partner to complete each task, and then answer the questions.

Review the photographs on pages 133 and 142 of *The Omnivore’s Dilemma*.

Read the text excerpts on pages 133–134 (stop at “. . . under federal organic rules.”), and pages 142–146 (starting at “Let us sell you lettuce.”).

1.  What information do the photographs give the viewer?

2.  They show toxic chemicals being added to the fields.

3.  They explain that the lettuce is produced in an industrial organic setting.

4.  <u>They show healthy-looking fields and packages of organic lettuce.</u>

5.  They explain that to produce the lettuce and get it to stores uses a lot of fossil fuels.

6.  What is one advantage of presenting this topic in print text format? (RI.8.7)

7.  <u>Students can return to the text and reread it as needed.</u>

8.  The speaker’s body language and tone help the reader understand the author’s purpose.

9.  It is easy for students to copy and paste parts of the document as a way of taking notes.

10. The text level matches all readers who encounter the text.

11. What is one disadvantage of presenting this topic in a photograph? (RI.8.7)

12. It is difficult for a photograph to be misinterpreted, so all viewers will have the same understanding presented about industrial organic farming.

13. <u>It gives an incomplete understanding to a viewer about the topic of industrial organic farming.</u>

14. The details can be overwhelming for a student who struggles with content of industrial organic farming or the level of the text.

15. Readers tend to rush and read text quickly, therefore not thinking deeply about the content of industrial organic farming.

Source: Pollan, Michael. *The Omnivore’s Dilemma* (Young Readers Edition). Penguin Young Readers, 2009.
