<td>Photo (Visual)</td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Does not require shared language or reading ability</p></li>
<li><p>Can be emotionally captivating</p></li>
<li><p>Provides visual details that can be hard to describe in text</p></li>
</ul></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Not always very specific</p></li>
<li><p>May rely too much on emotion and not enough on information</p></li>
<li><p>Does not provide background information</p></li>
<li><p>Can give an incomplete understanding to a viewer</p></li>
<li><p>Can be hard to identify author’s purpose</p></li>
<li><p>Can be misinterpreted</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td>Video (Multimedia: Visual and Audio)</td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Viewing information can positively impact engagement:</p></li>
<li><p>you are able to see characters in action, and</p></li>
<li><p>you can connect emotionally.</p></li>
<li><p>Comprehension is supported by seeing material presented visually.</p></li>
<li><p>Can go back and review information as needed</p></li>
<li><p>It can be personally convincing—watching or listening to someone speak may help the viewer trust the information.</p></li>
<li><p>Body language may reveal information about the speaker’s purpose.</p></li>
</ul></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Viewer has no control over the content and how, or by whom, it is presented.</p></li>
<li><p>The speed of presentation could impede a viewer’s initial understanding of the material presented.</p></li>
<li><p>It is difficult to “annotate” or use other active reading strategies while interacting with a video/interview.</p></li>
<li><p>Watching a video can be a passive activity and takes less thought and energy than actively reading (which requires thinking, reasoning, and making connections).</p></li>
<li><p>Because watching is more passive, engagement or attention span can be limited.</p></li>
<li><p>Speaker’s style may influence audience more than the content or information being presented.</p></li>
<li><p>If the style and tone of the video is very appealing, it could make it harder for the listener to objectively weigh the actual content.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Text (Printed Written Work)</td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Easy to distribute—can be copied or printed</p></li>
<li><p>Can be detailed and specific</p></li>
<li><p>Reader can gather evidence and information.</p></li>
<li><p>Reader can go back to the text multiple times.</p></li>
<li><p>Reader can employ active reading strategies (like annotating and coding).</p></li>
<li><p>Because it requires more time or attention from the viewer, it can promote deeper understanding.</p></li>
</ul></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Requires a shared language and reading ability</p></li>
<li><p>Requires more time or attention from the viewer</p></li>
<li><p>Reading level could be too high or low.</p></li>
<li><p>Details could be overwhelming.</p></li>
<li><p>There is no body language visible to help the reader understand more about the purpose of the text.</p></li>
<li><p>Readers tend to rush and read quickly, therefore not thinking deeply about a text.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td>Text (Digital Written Work)</td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Easy to distribute: can be shared, printed, emailed, or shared online</p></li>
<li><p>Easy to copy and paste parts of the document as a way of taking notes</p></li>
<li><p>Can be detailed and specific</p></li>
<li><p>Reader can go back to the text multiple times.</p></li>
<li><p>Reader can gather evidence and information using active strategies and online tools to code or annotate.</p></li>
</ul></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Requires a shared language and reading ability</p></li>
<li><p>Reading level could be too high or low</p></li>
<li><p>Details could be overwhelming</p></li>
<li><p>Details can be overwhelming to reader who struggles with content or text level</p></li>
<li><p>There is no body language visible to help the reader understand more about the purpose of the text</p></li>
<li><p>Readers tend to rush and read quickly, therefore not thinking deeply about a text</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Infographic (Multimedia: Visual and Written)</td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Visually compelling and engaging</p></li>
<li><p>Combination of captions, graphics, and other visuals can make information more straightforward.</p></li>
<li><p>It turns complicated information into visual graphics that are easy to understand.</p></li>
<li><p>Information is in “bite-sized” chunks and can be easier to retain.</p></li>
</ul></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Because the text is brief, it can be hard to build context and adequate understanding if background knowledge on the topic is limited.</p></li>
<li><p>It can leave the reader with unanswered questions.</p></li>
<li><p>Information can be misinterpreted.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td>Speech (Audio)</td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Does not require reading ability</p></li>
<li><p>Can be spoken with emotion and emphasis</p></li>
<li><p>Can enhance understanding of difficult or hard-to-pronounce words</p></li>
<li><p>Can be portable—can listen when on the go in the car, exercising, etc.</p></li>
</ul></td>
<td><ul>
<li><p>Can be hard to “go back to” or gather evidence from</p></li>
<li><p>Can be hard to have access to (need to be at the speech, or hear a recording)</p></li>
<li><p>Requires a shared language</p></li>
<li><p>Active reading strategies (like annotating) are not possible.</p></li>
<li><p>The style or voice of the speaker may deter some listeners.</p></li>
<li><p>If the style and tone of the speech is very appealing, it could make it harder for the listener to objectively weigh the actual content.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
