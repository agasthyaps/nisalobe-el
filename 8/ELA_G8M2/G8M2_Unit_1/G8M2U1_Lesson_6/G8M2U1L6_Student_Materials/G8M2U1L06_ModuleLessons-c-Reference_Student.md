Synopsis: *The Omnivore’s Dilemma*, Section 4

**Name:** **Date:**

**Pages 88–89**

- Companies use advertising to convince us that they’ve added value to the food they sell.

- Bright colors, added vitamins, and other methods are used to make people think the foods that companies sell are good for them.

**Pages 91–93**

- Companies encourage us to eat more food.

- Eating more food has caused an epidemic of obesity and diabetes, especially in children, which is killing us.

- High-fructose corn syrup is the main source of sweetener in our foods, which contains more calories.

**Pages 96–100**

- David Wallerstein created the concept of supersizing food in the 1950s.

- Wallerstein worked at a movie theater and realized that people were more likely to buy one large portion than buy two portions that are equal in size.

- He realized that people do not want to seem greedy.

- Humans have a sweet tooth and continue to eat sugar and fat even when they are full.

- Fattening foods are cheaper than low calorie foods, which affects poor people because they cannot afford the healthier food.

- The government creates the food pyramid to promote eating fruits and vegetables, but the government makes sure high-calorie foods are cheaper and low-calorie foods cost more.
