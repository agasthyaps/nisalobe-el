Synopsis: *The Omnivore’s Dilemma*, Section 5

**Name:** **Date:**

**Pages 133–134**

- Industrial organic food is grown without pesticides and chemical fertilizers.

- Much of this food is grown in large monoculture and industrial-/factory-type farms.

- Most organic milk is produced in factory farms.

- Most organic beef is raised in organic feedlots.

**Pages 147–150**

- Certain breeds of chicken are raised for use in the industrial food chain because they grow exceptionally fast.

- Free-range organic chickens often do not get much time outdoors.

- These chickens grow from egg to full size in seven weeks.

- This rapid growth and weight gain make it difficult for some chickens to continue standing and walking.

**Pages 153–155**

- Organic food can cost more.

- Organic food may taste better and does not contain chemicals.

- It is unclear if organic food is healthier. More tests are needed.

- Certain herbicides (used on corn that is fed to cows) are banned in Europe, yet permitted in the United States.
