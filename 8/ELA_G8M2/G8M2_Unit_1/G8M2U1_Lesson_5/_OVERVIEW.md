# Lesson 5: Delineate and Evaluate an Argument: Irrelevant Evidence and Conflicting Viewpoints
**Grade 8: Module 2: Unit 1: Lesson 5**

**Summary:** In this lesson, students continue to read their anchor text and evaluate the author’s argument by considering the relevance of evidence and if and how the author responds to conflicting viewpoints.

**Learning Targets:**
- I can distinguish between relevant and irrelevant evidence. (RI.8.8)
- I can evaluate an argument, assessing whether Michael Pollan’s evidence presented on the topic of industrial farming is relevant. (RI.8.8)
- I can evaluate an argument, analyzing how Michael Pollan responds to conflicting viewpoints. (RI.8.6)

**Agenda:** Opening → Engage the Learner – RI.8.8 (5 minutes) → Work Time → Sort Relevant and Irrelevant Evidence – RI.8.8 (10 minutes) → Irrelevant Evidence and Conflicting Viewpoints – RI.8.8 (20 minutes) → Closing and Assessment
