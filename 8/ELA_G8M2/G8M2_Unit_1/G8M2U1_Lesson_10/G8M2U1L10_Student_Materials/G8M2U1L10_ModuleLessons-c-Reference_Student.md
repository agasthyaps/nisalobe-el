Synopsis: *The Omnivore’s Dilemma*, Section 7

**Name:** **Date:**

**Pages 213–217**

- A diverse group of consumers buys its chicken and eggs from Polyface Farm.

- These consumers have various reasons for buying their chicken and eggs locally.

- Higher-quality food costs more to produce, so it costs more for the consumer.

- Many people will pay for high-quality appliances and clothing but resist the higher prices of high-quality food.

**Pages 219–223**

- There are natural growing seasons for crops and animals.

- Eating locally means getting used to eating seasonally.

- People can “vote with their forks” and make the word **local** as powerful as the word **organic** has become.

- Eating local is good for local businesses and the environment.

**Pages 229–231**

- Animals that eat grass gain high levels of important nutrients, and pass that, in turn, to the animal products we eat.

- Animals raised on grass have less fat and more healthy omega-3s and omega-6s.

- Eating corn-fed meat might be bad for your health.

- The type of food eaten by the animal you eat matters.
